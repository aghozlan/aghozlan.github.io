Exercise 1: Nutrition - Carbohydrates, Proteins, and Minerals (7 points)

Document 1: The Chemistry of a Balanced Breakfast

Carbohydrates and proteins are fundamental macronutrients required by the human body to sustain energy and repair tissue. During a typical breakfast, a student consumes milk and whole-grain bread. Whole grains are packed with starch, a complex carbohydrate made of long chains of glucose units. Milk supplies lactose (a disaccharide), alongside high-quality protein called casein. Casein provides essential amino acids linked together by peptide bonds. Furthermore, breakfast options are frequently fortified with minerals such as Calcium (Ca) and Iron (Fe) to optimize bone density and assist oxygen transport in red blood cells.

Exercise 2: Lipids and Vitamins - Energy Bars and Nutrient Stability (6 points)

Document 2: Fats and Micronutrient Behavior

Nutritional energy bars contain lipids as main energy reserves. These lipids are mostly triglycerides, formed by the reaction of glycerol with fatty acids. Fatty acids can be saturated (containing only single carbon-carbon bonds) or unsaturated. Additionally, these bars are enriched with vitamins. Vitamins are categorized based on their solubility: hydrosoluble vitamins (like Vitamin C) dissolve easily in water and are quickly excreted, while liposoluble vitamins (like Vitamin D) accumulate in the body's fatty tissues. A food laboratory monitored the degradation rate of Vitamin C and Vitamin D in energy bars when stored at room temperature over several months.

Exercise 3: Medical Application - Classification and Safety of Drugs (7 points)

Document 3: The Toolkit of Modern Medicine

Pharmaceutical drugs are essential for treating diseases and managing pain, but they must be classified correctly to avoid severe health complications.

Drug X (Aspirin): Acts as an analgesic to relieve mild-to-moderate pain, and also possesses anti-inflammatory action by reducing localized tissue swelling. However, it can cause stomach irritation by blocking protective gastric secretions.

Drug Y (Amoxicillin): Belongs to the family of antibiotics. It works selectively to destroy bacterial cells or stop their reproduction. It is completely ineffective against viruses, such as the common cold or influenza. Misuse of Drug Y by taking it without a medical prescription can lead to global bacterial resistance.
