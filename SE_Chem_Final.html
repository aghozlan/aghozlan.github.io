Exercise 1: Chemistry and Health - Managing Pain and Digestion (7 points)

Document 1: Aspirin and the Stomach

Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid) is one of the most widely used medications globally. It is commonly prescribed to relieve minor aches, reduce fever, and decrease inflammation. However, its acidic nature can irritate the gastric lining, sometimes leading to stomach ulcers if taken on an empty stomach. To mitigate this side effect, doctors often advise patients suffering from gastric hyperacidity to take an antacid, such as Maalox, alongside their medication. Maalox contains active ingredients like aluminum hydroxide Al(OH)3 and magnesium hydroxide Mg(OH)2, which act locally in the stomach to neutralize excess hydrochloric acid (HCl).

Exercise 2: Materials - The Journey of Plastics (6 points)

Document 2: The Environmental Impact of Polyethylene

Polyethylene (PE) is the most ubiquitous plastic globally, primarily used for packaging, plastic bags, and bottles. It is synthesized from ethene gas (C2H4) derived from petroleum refining. While PE has revolutionized industries due to its durability, low cost, and light weight, its resistance to chemical degradation has triggered a severe environmental crisis. In Lebanon, the mismanagement of plastic waste has led to the accumulation of PE in landfills and the Mediterranean Sea, threatening marine life. Recycling remains the most viable solution to mitigate this crisis.

2.1.
Indicate the name of the monomer used to synthesize Polyethylene (PE).
2.2.
Write the structural formula of the monomer C2H4, and specify if it is a saturated or unsaturated hydrocarbon.
2.3.
The synthesis of PE is an addition polymerization. Briefly explain what characterizes an addition polymerization reaction.
2.4.
Draw the repeating unit of the Polyethylene (PE) polymer.
2.5.
Based on Document 2, extract one advantage of using Polyethylene and one environmental problem caused by its disposal.
