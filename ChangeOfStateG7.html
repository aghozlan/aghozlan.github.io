Phase 1: ENGAGE

Boiling Water for Authentic Miramiyyeh (Sage Tea)

In many houses throughout Palestine and the UNRWA camps in Lebanon, our mornings start with traditional **Miramiyyeh** (sage tea). When we light the burner under a pot of cold water, we are initiating a beautiful physical sequence.

As energy passes from the flame into the kettle, the water temperature rises. Once it boils, we notice vigorous bubbles formed throughout the whole liquid, escaping as vapor. If we place a cool metal cover over the pot, we immediately see tiny droplets of liquid forming on the inside surface of the cover.

Evaporation vs Boiling

The steam rising slowly is evaporation. The rapid bubbles throughout are boiling.

Condensation Action

The hot steam hitting the cold metal lid loses energy, returning into sweet water drops.

Citizenship Connection: Conserving energy and resources in our community.

Phase 3: EXPLAIN

Terminology and Scientific Core

Let us break down the transitions we observed in the lab. Heat energy determines the physical state of a pure substance by regulating molecular vibration speed.

Solid ⇄ Liquid

Fusion & Solidification

**Fusion (Melting)** occurs when a solid absorbs thermal energy, turning into a liquid at its characteristic Melting Point (0°C for water). **Solidification (Freezing)** is the exact opposite—heat is released, causing the molecules to lock back into structural grids.

Pure water melting point = 0°C
Liquid ⇄ Gas

Boiling & Condensation

**Boiling** is a rapid state transition occurring throughout the entire volume of a liquid when heated to its Boiling Point (100°C for water). **Condensation** occurs when gas cools down (releases energy) and returns to liquid state.

Pure water boiling point = 100°C
Surface Transition

Evaporation vs Boiling

While both represent liquid-to-gas transitions, **Evaporation** is a slow process that happens only at the surface of a liquid at any temperature. **Boiling** happens rapidly, throughout the liquid volume, and only at boiling point.

• Evaporation: Quiet, surface, all temps.
• Boiling: Bubbles everywhere, specific temp.

Phase 4: ELABORATE & CITIZENSHIP

Water Security, Distillation & Resourcefulness

In UNRWA schools and camps within Lebanon, we often face challenges with water supply and quality. Understanding **change of state** is not just an academic exercise—it is a tool for survival, adaptation, and engineering solutions.

Did you know that pure water can be extracted from salty or contaminated water using simple solar distillation?

By trapping water under a plastic sheet warmed by the sun, the liquid water absorbs heat energy and **evaporates** (leaving the salts and impurities behind). As the water vapor rises and hits the cooler plastic dome, it **condenses** into clean droplets. Those droplets run down into a cup, providing pure drinking water.

Palestinian Resilience & Science Integration

Our ancestors preserved food and water through extreme seasons using state-change principles (like drying fruit and vegetables using pure evaporation, or condensing floral waters like orange blossom water—"Mazaher"). Our resourcefulness is our identity!

Reflective Community Problem Solving

Imagine there is an electrical power cut in Rashidieh Camp, and we cannot use refrigeration. To cool down a pot of fresh goat milk without ice, our grandmothers wrap the container in a wet cotton cloth and place it in the path of the wind.

Why does wrapping a container in a wet cloth cool down the contents inside?

That is correct! Because evaporation is an endothermic change of state (requires heat absorption), as the water molecules in the wet cloth evaporate, they pull heat away from the milk, keeping it naturally cooler! This is a simple, ecological thermodynamics trick.
Every observation we make connects us to the preservation of our environments.
