Exercise 1: Electrostatic Interactions & Charge Transfer (5 points)

Consider two identical conducting spheres $(A)$ and $(B)$, treated as point charges. They are initially fixed at a distance $d = 5.0\text{ cm}$ from each other. The charges carried by the spheres are $q_A = -6.0 \times 10^{-8}\text{ C}$ and $q_B = +2.0 \times 10^{-8}\text{ C}$ respectively.
Given: Coulomb's constant $k = 9.0 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$, Elementary charge $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$.

Figure 1: Spheres A and B separated by distance d

Part A: Initial State
1. State whether the electrostatic force between $(A)$ and $(B)$ is attractive or repulsive. Justify your answer.
2. Write the expression of the magnitude $F$ of the electric force exerted by $(A)$ on $(B)$, then calculate its value.
Part B: Contact and Equilibrium

The two spheres are unfastened and brought into contact. Once electric equilibrium is attained, they are separated and placed back at the same distance $d = 5.0\text{ cm}$. Let $q'_A$ and $q'_B$ be the new charges of $(A)$ and $(B)$.

1. Apply the principle of conservation of charge to calculate the value of the new charges $q'_A$ and $q'_B$.
2. Specify the direction of the transfer of electrons during the contact (from $A$ to $B$ or from $B$ to $A$). Justify.
3. Determine the number of electrons $N$ transferred between the two spheres.
Part C: Final State
1. Calculate the magnitude $F'$ of the new electrostatic force between $(A)$ and $(B)$.
2. To what distance $d'$ should the spheres be moved so that the new force $F''$ becomes equal to the original initial force $F$?

Exercise 2: Circuit Analysis, Network Laws & Charge Distribution (5 points)

A DC circuit consists of a battery $(G)$, a main switch $(K)$, and four lamps $L_1$, $L_2$, $L_3$, and $L_4$. The circuit branches at node $A$ and recombines at node $B$. A digital ammeter is connected in the main branch to monitor the total current. We assume the connecting wires have negligible resistance.

Figure 2: Diagram of the complex electric circuit

Part A: Current & Macroscopic Charge

Switch $K$ is closed. The digital ammeter, connected between the positive terminal and node $A$, displays a steady main current $I = 2.5\text{ A}$.

1. Calculate the quantity of electric charge $Q$ flowing through the main branch in a duration of $6.0\text{ minutes}$. Express the result in Coulombs (C).
2. Determine the total number of electrons crossing a cross-section of the main wire during this interval.
Part B: Voltage Distribution & Kirchhoff's Loop Rule

We choose the negative terminal $N$ as the reference of potentials ($V_N = 0\text{ V}$). The voltage across the battery is $U_{PN} = 12.0\text{ V}$. The voltage measured across lamp $L_4$ is $U_{BC} = 4.0\text{ V}$.

1. Determine the electric potential $V_P$ at the positive terminal, and deduce the potentials $V_A$ and $V_C$ at nodes $A$ and $C$.
2. Apply Kirchhoff's Voltage Law to find the voltage $U_{AB}$ across the parallel grouping of the branches between nodes $A$ and $B$.
3. Deduce the voltage across lamp $L_3$ and the total voltage across the series combination of $L_1$ and $L_2$.
4. If lamps $L_1$ and $L_2$ are identical, what is the exact voltage across $L_1$?
Part C: Current Distribution & Kirchhoff's Junction Rule

The current flowing through the middle branch containing $L_3$ is measured to be $I_3 = 1.5\text{ A}$.

1. Apply Kirchhoff's Current Law at node $A$ to deduce the value of the current $I_{12}$ flowing through the upper branch containing $L_1$ and $L_2$.
2. Knowing that $L_1$ and $L_3$ are completely identical lamps, compare their brightness. Justify your answer using the calculated currents.

Exercise 3: Characteristics of Dipoles & Operating Point (5 points)

A circuit consists of a generator $(G)$ of electromotive force $E$ and internal resistance $r$, connected in series with a motor $(M)$ of back electromotive force $e$ and internal resistance $r'$. Figure 3 represents the current-voltage $(U-I)$ characteristics of these two dipoles.

Figure 3: Current-Voltage Characteristics

Part A: Identification & Equations
1. Write the theoretical equation relating the voltage $U_G$ across a generator to $E$, $r$, and $I$.
2. Write the theoretical equation relating the voltage $U_M$ across a motor to $e$, $r'$, and $I$.
3. Specify which curve, (a) or (b), represents the generator. Justify your answer based on the slopes or initial values.
Part B: Extraction of Characteristics
1. Using Curve (a), determine the electromotive force $E$ of the generator and deduce its internal resistance $r$.
2. Using Curve (b), determine the back electromotive force $e$ of the motor and deduce its internal resistance $r'$.
Part C: Operating Point Analysis
1. Read the coordinates (current $I$ and voltage $U$) of the circuit's operating point from the graph.
2. Analytically verify the value of $I$ by applying Kirchhoff's voltage law (Pouillet's Law) to the single-loop series circuit.
3. Suppose the motor becomes completely blocked. State its new behavior, and calculate the new current $I'$ flowing through the circuit.

Exercise 4: Advanced Multiple Choice Questions (5 points)

For each of the following questions, select the single most accurate answer.

  1. If the distance separating two point charges is halved, the electrostatic force between them becomes:
    • Half as large.
    • Twice as large.
    • Four times as large.
    • One-fourth as large.
  2. When a neutral plastic rod is rubbed with a piece of wool and acquires a negative charge, this indicates that:
    • Protons were transferred from the rod to the wool.
    • Electrons were transferred from the wool to the rod.
    • Protons were transferred from the wool to the rod.
    • Electrons were transferred from the rod to the wool.
  3. The principle of conservation of electric charge is the foundational concept behind:
    • Ohm's Law.
    • Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (Loop Rule).
    • Kirchhoff's Current Law (Junction Rule).
    • Joule's Law.
  4. If four identical resistors, each of resistance $R$, are connected in parallel, the equivalent resistance of the grouping is:
    • $4R$
    • $R/4$
    • $R$
    • $R/16$
  5. An ideal voltmeter and an ideal ammeter should have internal resistances that are respectively:
    • Infinite and zero.
    • Zero and infinite.
    • Both infinite.
    • Both zero.
  6. The terminal voltage $U_{PN}$ of a generator discharging in a circuit is always:
    • Greater than its electromotive force $E$.
    • Equal to its electromotive force $E$.
    • Less than its electromotive force $E$.
    • Zero if the external circuit is purely resistive.
  7. The operating point of an electrical circuit consisting of a generator and a receiver is geometrically defined by:
    • The highest voltage point on the $y$-axis.
    • The intersection of their respective $U-I$ characteristic curves.
    • The maximum current point on the $x$-axis.
    • The average of their internal resistances.
  8. A motor acts as a pure ohmic resistor ($e=0$) when:
    • It rotates at its maximum speed.
    • It is disconnected from the generator.
    • Its rotor is physically blocked from turning.
    • It operates at maximum efficiency.
  9. Joule's effect refers to the phenomenon where electrical energy is transformed completely into:
    • Mechanical energy.
    • Chemical energy.
    • Thermal energy (heat).
    • Radiant energy (light).
  10. If two identical conducting spheres having initial charges of $+8\ \mu\text{C}$ and $-2\ \mu\text{C}$ touch and then separate, the final charge on each sphere will be:
    • $+6\ \mu\text{C}$
    • $+3\ \mu\text{C}$
    • $+5\ \mu\text{C}$
    • $-3\ \mu\text{C}$
