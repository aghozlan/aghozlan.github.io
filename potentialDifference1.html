DC Circuits: Potential Difference

Comprehensive Exercises & Marking Schemes

Syllabus Alignment: Lebanese Curriculum (Grade 10 / Seconde)

Sample 1: Series Circuit Principles

Consider the circuit diagram shown above. A dry cell maintains a constant voltage \( U_{PN} = 12 \text{ V} \). The connecting wires are ideal.

The voltage across lamp \( L_1 \) is measured to be \( U_{AB} = 4.5 \text{ V} \).

Contextual Questions:

  1. The node \( N \) is connected to the ground. Determine the electric potential of point \( N \) (\( V_N \)) and deduce the electric potential of point \( P \) (\( V_P \)).
  2. Indicate the values of the potential differences \( U_{PA} \) and \( U_{CN} \). Justify your answer.
  3. State the law of addition of voltages and use it to calculate the potential difference \( U_{BC} \) across lamp \( L_2 \).
  4. An oscilloscope is to be connected to measure \( U_{BC} \). Specify the connections required (Phase and Ground) to visualize a positive trace.

1. Electric Potentials [1.0 pt]

Since \( N \) is grounded, its electric potential is \( V_N = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt).

By definition: \( U_{PN} = V_P - V_N \) (0.25 pt).

\( 12 = V_P - 0 \implies V_P = 12 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

2. Ideal Wires [0.75 pt]

\( U_{PA} = 0 \text{ V} \) and \( U_{CN} = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt each) because the potential difference across an ideal connecting wire is zero (0.25 pt).

3. Law of Addition of Voltages [1.25 pts]

According to the law of addition of voltages in a series circuit:
\( U_{PN} = U_{PA} + U_{AB} + U_{BC} + U_{CN} \) (0.5 pt).

Substituting the values:
\( 12 = 0 + 4.5 + U_{BC} + 0 \) (0.25 pt)
\( U_{BC} = 12 - 4.5 = 7.5 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

4. Oscilloscope Connections [1.0 pt]

To visualize a positive trace (\( U_{BC} > 0 \)):

  • Connect the Phase (Y-input) to point B (0.5 pt).
  • Connect the Ground (Earth) to point C (0.5 pt).

Sample 2: Basic Mixed Circuit & Oscillograms

In the adjacent circuit, an oscilloscope is connected across the dry cell (\( U_{PN} \)). The resulting luminous horizontal line is displaced upwards by \( y = 3 \text{ divisions} \).

The vertical sensitivity of the oscilloscope is \( S_v = 3 \text{ V/div} \).

A digital voltmeter connected across lamp \( L_2 \) displays \( 5 \text{ V} \).

Contextual Questions:

  1. Calculate the value of the main voltage \( U_{PN} \).
  2. Deduce the voltage across lamp \( L_1 \) (\( U_{AB} \)). Name the physical law utilized.
  3. Determine the voltage across lamp \( L_3 \) (\( U_{DE} \)).
  4. Draw how to connect the terminals of the digital voltmeter (V and COM) on the schematic to measure \( U_{CD} \) displaying exactly \( +5 \text{ V} \). Describe this in text.

1. Oscilloscope Calculation [1.0 pt]

The formula for voltage from an oscillogram is \( U = S_v \times y \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{PN} = 3 \text{ V/div} \times 3 \text{ div} = 9 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

2. Law of Uniqueness of Voltages [1.0 pt]

Lamp \( L_1 \) is connected in parallel with the main branch (battery). Therefore, by the Law of Uniqueness of Voltages in parallel (0.5 pt):

\( U_{AB} = U_{PN} = 9 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

3. Voltage across L₃ [1.25 pts]

The lower branch (C to E) is also in parallel with the battery, so \( U_{CE} = U_{PN} = 9 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt).

Inside the lower branch, \( L_2 \) and \( L_3 \) are in series. Applying the law of addition of voltages:
\( U_{CE} = U_{CD} + U_{DE} \) (0.5 pt).

\( 9 = 5 + U_{DE} \implies U_{DE} = 4 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

4. Voltmeter Connections [0.75 pt]

To obtain a positive reading (\( U_{CD} = +5 \text{ V} \)):

  • The V terminal must be connected to the point of higher potential, which is C (closer to the positive pole P) (0.5 pt).
  • The COM terminal must be connected to point D (0.25 pt).

Sample 3: Advanced Mixed Circuit Analysis (8 Lamps)

Challenging

The complex circuit shown above consists of a dry cell and 8 incandescent lamps. Every lamp is situated exactly between two named nodes. The dry cell supplies a constant voltage \( U_{PN} = 24 \text{ V} \). Connecting wires are ideal.

Several node-specific voltage measurements have been recorded:

  • Voltage across \( L_1 \): \( U_{PA} = 4 \text{ V} \)
  • Voltage across \( L_2 \): \( U_{AD} = 6 \text{ V} \)
  • Voltage across \( L_5 \): \( U_{EF} = 7 \text{ V} \)
  • Voltage across \( L_6 \): \( U_{FB} = 5 \text{ V} \)
  • Voltage across \( L_7 \): \( U_{BC} = 5 \text{ V} \)

Contextual Questions:

  1. Deduce the voltage across lamp \( L_8 \). Justify your answer using the appropriate physical law.
  2. Apply the law of addition of voltages for the main loop to calculate the potential difference \( U_{AB} \) across the parallel block section bounded by nodes A and B.
  3. Calculate the voltage \( U_{DB} \) across lamp \( L_3 \).
  4. Calculate the voltage \( U_{AE} \) across lamp \( L_4 \).
  5. If Node N is grounded, determine the electric potentials at Nodes C, B, and A (\( V_C, V_B, V_A \)).

1. Law of Uniqueness in Parallel [1.0 pt]

Lamps \( L_7 \) and \( L_8 \) are connected in parallel strictly between nodes B and C. According to the law of uniqueness of voltages in a parallel circuit (0.5 pt), the voltage is the same across both branches.

\( U_{L8} = U_{L7} = U_{BC} = 5 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

2. Law of Addition of Voltages (Main Circuit) [1.5 pts]

For the entire main line traversing from P to N, we apply the law of addition of voltages:
\( U_{PN} = U_{PA} + U_{AB} + U_{BC} + U_{CN} \) (0.5 pt).

Since the connecting wires are ideal, \( U_{CN} = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt).

Substituting the values:
\( 24 = 4 + U_{AB} + 5 + 0 \) (0.25 pt)
\( 24 = 9 + U_{AB} \implies U_{AB} = 24 - 9 = 15 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

3. Voltage across L₃ (\( U_{DB} \)) [1.0 pt]

In the upper branch between A and B, lamps \( L_2 \) and \( L_3 \) are connected in series. Applying the law of addition of voltages:
\( U_{AB} = U_{AD} + U_{DB} \) (0.5 pt).

\( 15 = 6 + U_{DB} \implies U_{DB} = 15 - 6 = 9 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

4. Voltage across L₄ (\( U_{AE} \)) [1.0 pt]

In the lower branch between A and B, lamps \( L_4, L_5, \text{ and } L_6 \) are connected in series. Applying the law of addition of voltages:
\( U_{AB} = U_{AE} + U_{EF} + U_{FB} \) (0.5 pt).

\( 15 = U_{AE} + 7 + 5 \)
\( 15 = U_{AE} + 12 \implies U_{AE} = 3 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

5. Electric Potentials [1.5 pts]

If node N is grounded, \( V_N = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt).

\( U_{CN} = V_C - V_N = 0 \implies V_C = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.25 pt).

\( U_{BC} = V_B - V_C \implies 5 = V_B - 0 \implies V_B = 5 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{AB} = V_A - V_B \implies 15 = V_A - 5 \implies V_A = 20 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

Sample 4: Grounded Central Node & Negative Potentials

A circuit contains a battery and 6 lamps, arrayed such that every lamp is strictly situated between two named points. Point \( M \) is connected to the ground.

The battery provides a constant voltage \( U_{PN} = 24 \text{ V} \). The electric potential at node A is measured as \( V_A = 12 \text{ V} \), and the potential at node C is \( V_C = -4 \text{ V} \).

Additionally, the voltage across \( L_1 \) is \( U_{PA} = 2 \text{ V} \), and the voltage across \( L_4 \) is \( U_{BM} = 7 \text{ V} \).

Contextual Questions:

  1. Determine the voltage \( U_{AM} \) across lamp \( L_2 \).
  2. Deduce the voltage \( U_{AB} \) across lamp \( L_3 \).
  3. Calculate the electric potential at point P (\( V_P \)) and point N (\( V_N \)).
  4. Find the voltage \( U_{CN} \) across lamp \( L_6 \).

1. Voltage across L₂ (\( U_{AM} \)) [1.0 pt]

Since node M is grounded, its electric potential is \( V_M = 0 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{AM} = V_A - V_M = 12 - 0 = 12 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

2. Voltage across L₃ (\( U_{AB} \)) [1.0 pt]

The upper branch (A to B to M) is in parallel with lamp \( L_2 \) (A directly to M). Thus, the total voltage across the upper branch is \( U_{AM} = 12 \text{ V} \).

Applying the law of addition of voltages: \( U_{AM} = U_{AB} + U_{BM} \) (0.5 pt).

\( 12 = U_{AB} + 7 \implies U_{AB} = 5 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

3. Electric Potentials \( V_P \) and \( V_N \) [1.5 pts]

For \( V_P \): \( U_{PA} = V_P - V_A \implies 2 = V_P - 12 \implies V_P = 14 \text{ V} \) (0.75 pt).

For \( V_N \): The battery provides \( U_{PN} = 24 \text{ V} \). By definition, \( U_{PN} = V_P - V_N \).

\( 24 = 14 - V_N \implies V_N = 14 - 24 = -10 \text{ V} \) (0.75 pt).

4. Voltage across L₆ (\( U_{CN} \)) [1.0 pt]

Using the definition of potential difference: \( U_{CN} = V_C - V_N \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{CN} = -4 - (-10) = -4 + 10 = 6 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

Alternative approach: Main loop addition of voltages \( U_{PN} = U_{PA} + U_{AM} + U_{MC} + U_{CN} \) knowing \( U_{MC} = V_M - V_C = 0 - (-4) = 4 \text{ V} \) yields the same result.

Sample 5: Multi-loop Circuit with Measurement Devices

A mixed circuit comprises 6 lamps. An oscilloscope is connected across the parallel block (nodes A and C), displaying a positive waveform shifted by \( y = 4 \text{ div} \) with vertical sensitivity \( S_v = 2 \text{ V/div} \).

A digital voltmeter connected with its V terminal at B and its COM terminal at A displays a reading of \( -3 \text{ V} \).

Lamps \( L_4 \) and \( L_5 \) are identical, meaning they share the voltage equally (\( U_{AD} = U_{DC} \)). The voltages across \( L_1 \) and \( L_6 \) are known to be \( U_{PA} = 5 \text{ V} \) and \( U_{CN} = 7 \text{ V} \) respectively.

Contextual Questions:

  1. Calculate the total voltage \( U_{AC} \) across the parallel block.
  2. Determine the voltage \( U_{AB} \) across lamp \( L_2 \) and deduce the voltage \( U_{BC} \) across lamp \( L_3 \).
  3. Calculate the voltage \( U_{AD} \) across lamp \( L_4 \).
  4. Apply the law of addition of voltages to find the main voltage \( U_{PN} \) of the battery.

1. Oscilloscope Reading (\( U_{AC} \)) [1.0 pt]

The voltage indicated by the oscillogram is \( U = S_v \times y \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{AC} = 2 \text{ V/div} \times 4 \text{ div} = 8 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

2. Voltmeter Reading & \( U_{BC} \) [1.5 pts]

The voltmeter displays \( U_{\text{V-COM}} = U_{BA} = -3 \text{ V} \). Thus, \( U_{AB} = -U_{BA} = 3 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

In the upper branch, \( L_2 \) and \( L_3 \) are in series: \( U_{AC} = U_{AB} + U_{BC} \) (0.5 pt).

\( 8 = 3 + U_{BC} \implies U_{BC} = 5 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

3. Voltage across L₄ (\( U_{AD} \)) [1.0 pt]

In the lower branch, \( L_4 \) and \( L_5 \) are in series: \( U_{AC} = U_{AD} + U_{DC} \) (0.5 pt).

Since the lamps are identical, \( U_{AD} = U_{DC} \), meaning \( U_{AC} = 2 \times U_{AD} \).

\( 8 = 2 \times U_{AD} \implies U_{AD} = 4 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).

4. Main Battery Voltage (\( U_{PN} \)) [1.0 pt]

Applying the law of addition of voltages across the entire main loop from P to N:
\( U_{PN} = U_{PA} + U_{AC} + U_{CN} \) (0.5 pt).

\( U_{PN} = 5 + 8 + 7 = 20 \text{ V} \) (0.5 pt).
