Exercise 1: Kinetics of Iodide Oxidation (5 points)

In aqueous solution, peroxydisulfate ions ($S_2O_8^{2-}$) slowly oxidize iodide ions ($I^-$) to form iodine ($I_2$) and sulfate ions ($SO_4^{2-}$). At time $t = 0$, we mix a volume $V_1 = 50.0 \text{ mL}$ of potassium iodide solution ($K^+_{(aq)} + I^-_{(aq)}$) of concentration $C_1 = 0.20 \text{ mol/L}$ with a volume $V_2 = 50.0 \text{ mL}$ of sodium peroxydisulfate solution ($2Na^+_{(aq)} + S_2O_8^{2-}_{(aq)}$) of concentration $C_2 = 0.05 \text{ mol/L}$. QDetailed Answer Key & Logical StepsPts
1 Analysis: From Document 4, initially the amounts of $N_2$ and $H_2$ decrease while $NH_3$ increases. Starting from time $t_e$, the curves become horizontal (parallel to the time axis). The amounts stabilize at non-zero values.
Proof: The simultaneous presence of all reactants and products at constant, non-zero amounts after $t_e$ proves that the forward and reverse reaction rates have become equal. The system has reached a state of dynamic chemical equilibrium. 		1.5
2 ICE Table tracking:
Initial: $n(N_2) = 2.0$, $n(H_2) = 6.0$, $n(NH_3) = 0$
Change: $-x$, $-3x$, $+2x$
Equilibrium moles: $n(N_2)_{eq} = 2.0 - x$, $n(H_2)_{eq} = 6.0 - 3x$, $n(NH_3)_{eq} = 2x$
Concentrations ($V=2.0\text{ L}$):
$[N_2] = \frac{2.0 - x}{2.0}$; $[H_2] = \frac{6.0 - 3x}{2.0}$; $[NH_3] = \frac{2x}{2.0} = x$ 		1
3 Calculation: At equilibrium, $n(NH_3) = 2.0 \text{ mol}$. Therefore, $2x = 2.0 \Rightarrow x = 1.0 \text{ mol}$.
Equilibrium concentrations:
$[N_2] = \frac{2.0 - 1.0}{2.0} = 0.5 \text{ mol/L}$
$[H_2] = \frac{6.0 - 3(1.0)}{2.0} = 1.5 \text{ mol/L}$
$[NH_3] = \frac{2.0}{2.0} = 1.0 \text{ mol/L}$
$K_c = \frac{[NH_3]^2}{[N_2][H_2]^3} = \frac{(1.0)^2}{(0.5)(1.5)^3} = \frac{1}{0.5 \times 3.375} = \frac{1}{1.6875} \approx 0.593$. 		1.5
4 Deduction: The equilibrium will shift in the forward direction (to the right).
Justification: According to Le Chatelier’s principle, an increase in overall pressure forces the system to shift in the direction that reduces pressure, which is the direction producing fewer moles of gas. The forward reaction goes from 4 moles of gas ($1 N_2 + 3 H_2$) to 2 moles of gas ($2 NH_3$). Therefore, the shift is forward. 		1

Justification:
1. It allows the mixture to be heated (increasing the rate of the slow reaction).
2. The vertical condenser cools the vapors back into liquids, returning them to the flask. This prevents any loss of volatile reactants (ethanol) and products (ester) to the atmosphere. 		1.5
3 Explanation of "Limited": The reaction is reversible. As the ester and water form, they react together in the reverse hydrolysis reaction. An equilibrium is reached where all species are present, meaning the reactants are never fully consumed ($100\%$ yield is impossible).
Thermodynamic nature: Athermic ($\Delta H \approx 0$). 		1
4 Calculation:
The theoretical maximum amount of ester (if the reaction were complete) is equal to the initial amount of the limiting reactant. Since the mixture is equimolar ($0.4 \text{ mol}$ of each), theoretical yield $n_{th} = 0.4 \text{ mol}$.
The actual experimental amount formed is $n_{exp} = 0.264 \text{ mol}$.
Yield percentage = $(n_{exp} / n_{th}) \times 100$
Yield = $(0.264 / 0.4) \times 100 = 66\%$. 		1.5
