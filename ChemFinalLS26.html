3 Definition: The half-life t1/2 is the time required for the concentration of the limiting reactant to drop to half its initial value, which corresponds to the formation of half the maximum concentration of the product.
Graphical Determination: The maximum concentration is [I2]max = 10.0 mmol/L (asymptote).
At t = t1/2, [I2] = 10.0 / 2 = 5.0 mmol/L. Projecting 5.0 mmol/L onto the curve and down to the time axis gives t1/2 = 10 min. 		1
4 Analysis: The slope of the tangent to the curve represents the rate of formation of I2. Graphically, the slope of the tangent continuously decreases over time, indicating that the rate of formation decreases until it reaches zero at the plateau.
Microscopic Explanation: As the reaction proceeds, the reactants are consumed. The decrease in reactant concentrations lowers the probability of effective collisions between reactant molecules per unit volume and time, thereby decreasing the reaction rate. 		1.5

Exercise 3: Methanol Synthesis and Chemical Equilibrium (5 pts)

QDetailed Answer Key & Logical StepsPts
1 Analysis: From Document 4, the amounts of reactants (CO and H2) decrease while the product (CH3OH) increases initially. Starting from time te, the curves become strictly horizontal (parallel to the time axis). The amounts stabilize at non-zero constant values (CO=1.0, H2=2.0, CH3OH=2.0).
Proof: The simultaneous presence of all reactants and products at constant, non-zero amounts after te proves that the forward and reverse reaction rates are equal and not zero. The system has reached a dynamic chemical equilibrium. 		1
2 ICE Table tracking:
Initial (mol): n(CO) = 3.0, n(H2) = 6.0, n(CH3OH) = 0
Change (mol): -x, -2x, +x
Equilibrium (mol): 3.0 - x, 6.0 - 2x, x
Proof of x: From Doc 4, the equilibrium amount of H2 is 2.0 mol. Therefore, 6.0 - 2x = 2.0 ⇒ 2x = 4.0 ⇒ x = 2.0 mol. (Alternatively using CO: 3.0 - x = 1.0 ⇒ x = 2.0 mol). 		1.5
3 Calculation: At equilibrium, x = 2.0 mol.
Equilibrium amounts: n(CO) = 1.0 mol, n(H2) = 2.0 mol, n(CH3OH) = 2.0 mol.
Equilibrium concentrations (V = 2.0 L):
[CO] = 1.0 / 2.0 = 0.5 mol/L
[H2] = 2.0 / 2.0 = 1.0 mol/L
[CH3OH] = 2.0 / 2.0 = 1.0 mol/L
Kc = [CH3OH] / ([CO][H2]2) = 1.0 / (0.5 × 1.02) = 1.0 / 0.5 = 2.0. 		1.5
4 Deduction: The equilibrium will shift in the forward direction (to the right).
Justification: According to Le Chatelier’s principle, a decrease in temperature forces the system to shift in the direction that releases heat to oppose the cooling. Since the forward synthesis reaction is exothermic (ΔH < 0), the system shifts in the forward direction. 		1

Exercise 4: Organic Chemistry - Esterification Reaction (5 pts)

QDetailed Answer Key & Logical StepsPts
1 Equation:
CH3-COOH + CH3-CH2-OH &#rightleftharpoons; CH3-COO-CH2-CH3 + H2O
Note: The double arrow (&#rightleftharpoons;) is mandatory for full credit. 		1
2 Name: Heating under reflux apparatus (Montage à reflux).
Justifications:
1. Heating: Increases the temperature to accelerate the intrinsically slow reaction.
2. Reflux (Condenser): Condenses the vapors back into the flask to prevent the loss of volatile reactants (ethanol) and products (ester) to the atmosphere. 		1.5
3 In addition to being athermic, the esterification reaction is slow and limited (reversible, does not reach 100% completion). 1
4 Calculation:
The theoretical maximum amount of ester nth (if reaction were complete) equals the initial amount of the limiting reactant. Since the mixture is equimolar (0.4 mol of each), nth = 0.4 mol.
The actual experimental amount formed is nexp = 0.264 mol.
Yield percentage = (nexp / nth) × 100 = (0.264 / 0.4) × 100 = 66%.
Method to increase yield: Continuously remove one of the products (e.g., extracting water using a Dean-Stark apparatus or distilling the ester) to shift the equilibrium in the forward direction according to Le Chatelier's principle. 		1.5
