Exercise: Comprehensive Study of a Coil (7 points)

A solenoid \((C)\) is characterized by its number of turns \(N = 500\), its constant cross-sectional area \(S = 20 \text{ cm}^2\), its inductance \(L\), and its internal resistance \(r\). The aim of this exercise is to study the phenomena of electromagnetic induction and self-induction using this identical solenoid.

Part A: Electromagnetic Induction

The solenoid \((C)\) is connected in series with a resistor of resistance \(R_1 = 8\ \Omega\). The equivalent resistance of the entire closed circuit is \(R_{eq} = R_1 + r\). The solenoid is placed in a uniform magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) parallel to its axis of revolution. The positive direction of the normal \(\vec{n}\) to the surface of the turns is chosen such that the angle between \(\vec{n}\) and \(\vec{B}\) is \(\alpha = 0^\circ\).

The magnitude of the magnetic field varies with time according to the equation: \(B(t) = -0.05 t + 0.2\) (expressed in SI units), for \(t \ge 0\).

  • 1. Magnetic Flux
    • 1.1. Write the expression of the magnetic flux \(\Phi_1\) traversing a single turn of the solenoid.
    • 1.2. Deduce the expression of the total magnetic flux \(\Phi\) traversing the solenoid \((C)\) as a function of time \(t\).
  • 2. Induced Electromotive Force
    • 2.1. State Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction.
    • 2.2. Determine the algebraic value of the induced electromotive force \(e\) in the solenoid.
  • 3. Induced Current
    • 3.1. The total equivalent resistance of the circuit is experimentally measured to be \(R_{eq} = 10\ \Omega\). Deduce the value of the internal resistance \(r\) of the solenoid.
    • 3.2. Calculate the value of the induced current \(i\).
    • 3.3. State Lenz's law. Based on this law, does the induced current create an induced magnetic field \(\vec{B_{ind}}\) in the same direction as \(\vec{B}\) or opposite to it? Justify your answer.
Part B: Self-Induction

  • A resistor of resistance \(R_2 = 18\ \Omega\).
  • A switch \(K\).

At the instant \(t = 0\), the switch \(K\) is closed.

  • 1. Differential Equation

    • 1.2. The steady-state current is denoted by \(I_0\). Deduce the theoretical expression of \(I_0\) in terms of \(E\), \(r\), and \(R_2\), and calculate its numerical value.
  • 2. Study of the Time Constant
