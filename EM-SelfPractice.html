Exercise 1: Electromagnetic Induction - The Moving Rod (10 points)

Two parallel horizontal conducting rails, separated by a distance \(L = 20 \text{ cm}\), are connected at one end by a resistor of resistance \(R = 5\ \Omega\). A conducting rod \(MN\) of negligible resistance is placed across the rails. The entire setup is placed in a uniform, vertical magnetic field \(\vec{B}\) pointing into the page, with a magnitude \(B = 0.5 \text{ T}\).

An external mechanical force pulls the rod \(MN\) to the right with a constant velocity \(\vec{v}\) of magnitude \(v = 4 \text{ m/s}\). At the instant \(t = 0\), the rod is at a distance \(x_0 = 0\) from the resistor. Let \(x\) be the position of the rod at any instant \(t\). The positive direction of the normal \(\vec{n}\) is chosen downwards (into the page), parallel to \(\vec{B}\).

Figure 1: Conducting Rod Moving on Rails
Part A: Kinematics and Magnetic Flux
  • 1. Study of the Flux
    • 1.1. Express the area \(S\) of the circuit formed by the rails, the resistor, and the rod at an instant \(t\), in terms of \(L\), \(v\), and \(t\).
    • 1.2. Deduce the expression of the magnetic flux \(\Phi(t)\) traversing the circuit. Indicate whether the flux is increasing or decreasing over time.
    • 1.3. Applying Faraday's Law, determine the algebraic value of the induced electromotive force \(e\).
    • 1.4. State Lenz's Law. Based on this law, deduce the real direction of the induced current passing through the rod \(MN\).
Part B: Electrical and Mechanical Power
  • 2. Forces and Energy Conservation
    • 2.1. Determine the magnitude of the induced current \(i\) flowing in the circuit.
    • 2.2. The current-carrying rod \(MN\) is subjected to a magnetic Laplace force \(\vec{F_L}\). Specify the direction of this force and calculate its magnitude.
    • 2.3. To maintain the constant velocity \(\vec{v}\), an external agent applies a mechanical force \(\vec{F_{ext}}\). Assuming friction is negligible, state the relation between \(\vec{F_{ext}}\) and \(\vec{F_L}\).
    • 2.4. Calculate the mechanical power \(P_m\) provided by the external force, and verify that it is entirely converted into electrical power \(P_J\) dissipated by Joule effect in the resistor \(R\).


Exercise 2: Self-Induction - The RL Circuit (10 points)

A circuit is set up to study the establishment of current in a coil. The circuit, shown in Figure 2, consists of perfectly connected components:

  • A DC ideal voltage generator of electromotive force \(E = 12 \text{ V}\).
  • An ohmic resistor of resistance \(R = 40\ \Omega\).
  • A coil of inductance \(L = 0.5 \text{ H}\) and internal resistance \(r = 10\ \Omega\).
  • A switch \(K\).

At the instant \(t = 0\), the switch \(K\) is closed, completing the circuit. The direction of the positive current \(i\) is chosen to be the physical direction of the current delivered by the generator.

Figure 2: RL Series Circuit (Switch closed at t=0)
Part A: Differential Equation and Time Constant
  • 1. Mathematical Modeling of the Circuit
    • 1.1. Applying the law of addition of voltages, derive the first-order differential equation that governs the evolution of the current \(i(t)\).
    • 1.2. When the steady state is reached, the current attains a constant maximum value denoted by \(I_0\). Deduce the literal expression of \(I_0\) and calculate its numerical value.
    • 1.3. The solution to the differential equation is of the form \(i(t) = I_0 (1 - e^{-t/\tau})\), where \(\tau\) is the time constant of the circuit. Substitute this solution back into the differential equation to prove that \(\tau = \frac{L}{R + r}\).
Part B: Experimental Observation and Energy
  • 2. Oscilloscope and Magnetic Energy

    An oscilloscope is connected to the circuit to visualize the voltage \(u_R\) across the resistor \(R\) on channel \((Y_1)\).

    • 2.1. Redraw Figure 2 on your answer sheet. Clearly indicate the connections of the oscilloscope (Ground and phase \(Y_1\)) required to display the voltage \(u_R(t)\).
    • 2.2. Explain briefly why analyzing the oscillogram of \(u_R(t)\) is strictly equivalent to analyzing the variations of the current \(i(t)\).
    • 2.3. Calculate the exact theoretical value of the time constant \(\tau\) for this specific circuit.
    • 2.4. Determine the amount of magnetic energy \(E_m\) stored in the coil when the steady state is fully established.
