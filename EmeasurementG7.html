⚡ How do we know what's happening inside a circuit?
Electric current is invisible — but we can measure it! Two special instruments let us see exactly what electricity is doing. Let's discover them.

🔗 Ammeter — Series Connection
The ammeter is placed in the main path of the circuit so that all the current flows through it. It has very low resistance so it doesn't affect the current.
Same current enters and exits the ammeter
⚡ Voltmeter — Parallel Connection
The voltmeter is connected across (beside) the component you want to measure. It has very high resistance so almost no current flows through it.
Measures the potential difference across two points
⚠ Never connect an ammeter in parallel — it can damage the instrument due to its very low resistance. Never connect a voltmeter in series — it has very high resistance and the reading will be nearly zero.

Explain — Symbols & Circuit Rules

Master the official circuit symbols and connection rules

EXPLAIN
🔍 Reading Circuit Diagrams
In circuit diagrams, every component has a standard symbol. The ammeter and voltmeter each have their own — a circle with a capital letter inside.
CIRCUIT DIAGRAM — COMPLETE CIRCUIT WITH BOTH METERS
Standard circuit diagram: Battery → Ammeter (series) → Bulb with Voltmeter (parallel)
Key Symbol Rules:
• Ammeter (A in circle) — drawn IN the wire, breaks the circuit like a door
• Voltmeter (V in circle) — drawn BESIDE the component, with two wires connecting across it
• Both symbols: circle with capital letter — ⓐ and ⓥ
• The connecting wires in diagrams are always straight lines at right angles
