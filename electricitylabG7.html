E2 · Materials

Conductors & Insulators

Not all materials allow electricity to pass through them. Understanding the difference is key to safe circuit design.

⚡ CONDUCTOR
A material that allows electric current to flow through it easily.
Why? It has free electrons that can move.
Examples: Copper, aluminium, iron, silver, water (impure)
🚫 INSULATOR
A material that does NOT allow electric current to flow through it.
Why? Its electrons are tightly bound — cannot move freely.
Examples: Rubber, plastic, glass, wood, ceramic, dry air

E3 · Switch

The Switch — Open & Closed

A switch controls the flow of current by either completing or breaking the circuit. Tap the switches to see what happens.

🔓 Open Switch
The circuit is broken. No current can flow.
The lamp goes off. Energy cannot travel around the loop.
🔒 Closed Switch
The circuit is complete. Current flows freely.
The lamp lights up. Energy travels from generator → receiver.
True or False
When a switch is open, current still flows but very weakly.
ATRUE — the current is just reduced
BFALSE — an open switch completely stops current flow
