Exercise 1  —  Hereditary Phenylketonuria (PKU) 5 points

Phenylketonuria (PKU) is an autosomal recessive hereditary disease caused by a deficiency of the enzyme phenylalanine hydroxylase (PAH), which converts phenylalanine (Phe) into tyrosine in liver cells. When PAH is deficient or abnormal, phenylalanine accumulates to toxic levels in the blood. Let P represent the symbol of the normal (dominant) allele and p the symbol of the mutated (recessive) allele.

Phenylketonuria can arise from two different molecular origins:

Origin 1: A mutation leading to the production of a reduced quantity of PAH enzyme in liver cells.

Origin 2: A mutation leading to the production of a structurally abnormal PAH enzyme (present in normal quantity but non-functional) in liver cells.

Exercise 2  —  Spermatogenesis 5 points

Spermatogenesis is the process by which the seminiferous tubules of the testes produce spermatozoa. It lasts approximately 72 days and occurs continuously starting from puberty. It has four successive phases: multiplication, growth, maturation, and spermiogenesis. Spermatogenesis ensures male fertility and contributes directly to the genetic transmission of hereditary traits to offspring.
