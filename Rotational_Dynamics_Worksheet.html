Exercise 1

A homogeneous rod of length L = 1 m and weight W = 10 N is free to rotate about a horizontal axis \((\Delta)\) passing through the point O. Two forces F\(_1\) = 2 N and F\(_2\) = 3 N are applied respectively at the extremities A and B of the rod as shown on the figure.

Given: OG = 20 cm.

  1. Calculate the total moment of all forces applied on the rod.
  2. Is the rod at equilibrium? If no, in which direction will it rotate?

Detailed Solution

Preliminary Analysis & Distances:
Let's define the positions based on the diagram. The center of gravity \(G\) is at the middle of the rod.
Length \(L = 1 \text{ m} \implies AG = GB = 0.5 \text{ m}\).
Given \(OG = 20 \text{ cm} = 0.2 \text{ m}\). Since \(O\) is between \(G\) and \(B\):
Distance \(OA = AG + OG = 0.5 + 0.2 = 0.7 \text{ m}\).
Distance \(OB = GB - OG = 0.5 - 0.2 = 0.3 \text{ m}\).
We choose the counter-clockwise (CCW) direction as the positive sense of rotation.
1) Total moment of all forces applied on the rod:
The forces acting on the rod are the weight \(\vec{W}\), the applied forces \(\vec{F}_1\) and \(\vec{F}_2\), and the reaction \(\vec{R}\) at the pivot \(O\).
  • Moment of Weight (\(\vec{W}\)): Acts downward at \(G\). It causes a counter-clockwise rotation around \(O\).
    \(M_{W} = + W \times OG = 10 \times 0.2 = +2.0 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
  • Moment of \(\vec{F}_1\): Acts downward at \(B\). It causes a clockwise rotation around \(O\).
    \(M_{F_1} = - F_1 \times OB = -2 \times 0.3 = -0.6 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
  • Moment of \(\vec{F}_2\): Acts at \(A\) making an angle of \(60^\circ\) with the rod, pulling upwards and leftwards. Its perpendicular component is \(F_2 \sin(60^\circ)\). It causes a clockwise rotation around \(O\).
    \(M_{F_2} = - F_2 \sin(60^\circ) \times OA = -3 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times 0.7 \approx -1.818 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
  • Moment of Reaction (\(\vec{R}\)): Acts at the axis \(O\).
    \(M_{R} = 0 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
\(\sum M = 2.0 - 0.6 - 1.818 = -0.418 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
2) Equilibrium status and rotation direction:
Since the sum of moments \(\sum M = -0.418 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \neq 0\), the rod is not at equilibrium.
Because the total moment is negative, the rod will rotate in the negative direction, which is clockwise.

Exercise 2

The painter of mass m\(_1\) = 82 kg shown in the adjacent figure is working between two parallel walls on a stand where AD = 2.8 m and of mass m\(_2\) = 24 kg. The painter is standing at a point B so that AB = 1 m. The stand is hinged horizontally to one wall by a horizontal axis at A while the other end is connected to a cable taken massless and fixed to a ring R\(_2\) on the other wall.

The paint bucket has a mass m\(_3\) = 16 kg and is placed at a point C on the stand so that AC = 2 m. Take g = 10 m/s\(^2\).

  1. Draw a diagram showing the forces acting on the stand.
  2. Determine the magnitude of the tension \(\vec{T}\) exerted by the cable at D.
  3. Determine the magnitude and the direction of the reaction of support \(\vec{R}\) exerted by the axis at A.
  4. Knowing that this cable can withstand at maximum tension of T\(_{max}\) = 1392 N. Determine the maximum distance from A at which the painter can stand before cutting the cable.

Detailed Solution

Weights Calculation:
Painter: \(W_1 = m_1 g = 82 \times 10 = 820 \text{ N}\) (acts at \(x = 1 \text{ m}\))
Stand: \(W_2 = m_2 g = 24 \times 10 = 240 \text{ N}\) (acts at center of gravity, \(x = 1.4 \text{ m}\))
Bucket: \(W_3 = m_3 g = 16 \times 10 = 160 \text{ N}\) (acts at \(x = 2 \text{ m}\))
1) Free Body Diagram:
(Instruction to student: Sketch the horizontal stand AD. Draw \(W_1\) downward at \(B\), \(W_2\) downward at the midpoint, \(W_3\) downward at \(C\). Draw Tension \(T\) at \(D\) pulling up and right at \(30^\circ\). Draw Reaction \(R\) at \(A\) with arbitrary \(R_x\) and \(R_y\) components.)
2) Magnitude of Tension \(\vec{T}\):
Apply the condition for rotational equilibrium: \(\sum M_A = 0\)
Let counter-clockwise be positive.
\(M_T - M_{W_1} - M_{W_2} - M_{W_3} = 0\)
\((T \sin 30^\circ \times 2.8) - (820 \times 1) - (240 \times 1.4) - (160 \times 2) = 0\)
\(1.4T - 820 - 336 - 320 = 0\)
\(1.4T = 1476\)
\(T = \frac{1476}{1.4} \approx 1054.29 \text{ N}\)
3) Magnitude and Direction of Reaction \(\vec{R}\):
Apply translational equilibrium: \(\sum \vec{F} = 0\)
X-axis: \(R_x - T \cos 30^\circ = 0 \implies R_x = 1054.29 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 913.04 \text{ N}\)
Y-axis: \(R_y + T \sin 30^\circ - W_1 - W_2 - W_3 = 0\)
\(R_y + (1054.29 \times 0.5) - 820 - 240 - 160 = 0\)
\(R_y + 527.15 - 1220 = 0 \implies R_y = 692.85 \text{ N}\)
Magnitude: \(R = \sqrt{R_x^2 + R_y^2} = \sqrt{913.04^2 + 692.85^2}\)
Magnitude \(R \approx 1146.16 \text{ N}\)
Direction \(\theta = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{R_y}{R_x}\right) = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{692.85}{913.04}\right) \approx 37.2^\circ\) above the horizontal.
4) Maximum Distance for the Painter:
Let \(x_{max}\) be the maximum distance from A.
Using maximum tension \(T_{max} = 1392 \text{ N}\) in the moment equation:
\((1392 \times \sin 30^\circ \times 2.8) = (820 \times x_{max}) + 336 + 320\)
\(696 \times 2.8 = 820 x_{max} + 656\)
\(1948.8 = 820 x_{max} + 656\)
\(820 x_{max} = 1292.8\)
\(x_{max} = \frac{1292.8}{820} \approx 1.577 \text{ m}\)

Exercise 3

A system of wheels of different radii are bonded together and rotate in the vertical plane about a common horizontal frictionless axle passing through their common center O. Three forces are acting on the wheels as shown in the adjacent figure.

The radii of small wheel and large wheel are R\(_1\) = 20 cm and R\(_2\) = 40 cm respectively. The moment of inertia of the system about the rotation axle is I = 0.2 kg·m\(^2\).

Given that: F\(_1\) = 6 N, F\(_2\) = 9 N, F\(_3\) = 4 N, \(\sin(\alpha)\) = 0.94.
The system starts from rest at t\(_0\) = 0.

  1. Apply Newton's 2\(^{nd}\) law for rotation to determine the angular acceleration of the wheels. Indicate the sense of rotation.
  2. Determine, at t = 10 s, the angular speed of the wheel and the number of turns accomplished.
  3. At t = 10 s, a braking force \(\vec{F}_b\) is applied tangentially to the large wheel so that the wheels decelerate uniformly and stop after making 10 turns.
    3.1) Determine the new angular acceleration.
    3.2) Deduce the time needed by the wheels to stop during this phase of motion.
    3.3) Determine the magnitude of braking force \(\vec{F}_b\).

Detailed Solution

1) Newton's 2\(^{nd}\) Law for Rotation (\(\sum M = I \ddot{\theta}\)):
Let counter-clockwise (CCW) be the positive sense of rotation (as indicated in the figure's positive direction arc).
Radii: \(R_1 = 0.2 \text{ m}\), \(R_2 = 0.4 \text{ m}\).
  • \(M_{F_2} = + F_2 \times R_2 = 9 \times 0.4 = +3.6 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\) (CCW)
  • \(M_{F_1} = - F_1 \times R_2 = -6 \times 0.4 = -2.4 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\) (CW)
  • \(M_{F_3} = - F_3 \sin(\alpha) \times R_1 = -4 \times 0.94 \times 0.2 = -0.752 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\) (CW)
\(\sum M = 3.6 - 2.4 - 0.752 = 0.448 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}\)
Using \( \sum M = I \ddot{\theta} \):
\( 0.448 = 0.2 \ddot{\theta} \implies \ddot{\theta} = \frac{0.448}{0.2} = 2.24 \text{ rad/s}^2\)
Since \(\ddot{\theta} > 0\), the system rotates in the counter-clockwise direction.
2) Angular speed and number of turns at t = 10 s:
Angular speed: \(\omega = \ddot{\theta} t + \omega_0 = (2.24 \times 10) + 0\)
Angular displacement: \(\Delta\theta = \frac{1}{2} \ddot{\theta} t^2 = 0.5 \times 2.24 \times (10)^2 = 112 \text{ rad}\)
Angular speed \(\omega = 22.4 \text{ rad/s}\)
Number of turns \(N = \frac{\Delta\theta}{2\pi} = \frac{112}{2\pi} \approx 17.83 \text{ turns}\)
3.1) New angular acceleration:
The system decelerates to stop (\(\omega_f = 0\)) over \(\Delta\theta' = 10 \text{ turns} = 20\pi \text{ rad} \approx 62.83 \text{ rad}\).
Initial speed for this phase \(\omega_i = 22.4 \text{ rad/s}\).
Using time-independent kinematic equation: \(\omega_f^2 - \omega_i^2 = 2 \ddot{\theta}_{new} \Delta\theta'\)
\(0 - (22.4)^2 = 2 \times \ddot{\theta}_{new} \times (20\pi) \implies -501.76 = 125.66 \ddot{\theta}_{new}\)
\(\ddot{\theta}_{new} \approx -3.99 \text{ rad/s}^2\)
3.2) Time to stop:
Using \(\omega_f = \ddot{\theta}_{new} t' + \omega_i\)
\(0 = -3.99 t' + 22.4 \implies t' = \frac{22.4}{3.99} \approx 5.61 \text{ s}\)
3.3) Magnitude of braking force \(F_b\):
Assuming the driving forces are still acting during braking, the new net moment is the original net moment minus the torque created by the braking force (\(F_b\) acts on large wheel tangentially).
\(\sum M_{new} = I \ddot{\theta}_{new} \implies \sum M_{original} - (F_b \times R_2) = I \ddot{\theta}_{new}\)
\(0.448 - (F_b \times 0.4) = 0.2 \times (-3.99)\)
\(0.448 - 0.4 F_b = -0.798\)
\(0.4 F_b = 0.448 + 0.798 = 1.246 \implies F_b = \frac{1.246}{0.4} = 3.115 \text{ N}\)
(Note: If the problem assumes the original driving forces are removed during the braking phase, the equation simplifies to \(-F_b \times 0.4 = -0.798 \implies F_b \approx 1.995 \text{ N}\))
