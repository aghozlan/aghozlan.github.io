Chapter X: ROTATIONAL DYNAMICS

Exercise 1

A homogeneous rod of length \( L = 1 \text{ m} \) and weight \( W = 10 \text{ N} \) is free to rotate about a horizontal axis \( (\Delta) \) passing through the point O. Two forces \( F_1 = 2 \text{ N} \) and \( F_2 = 3 \text{ N} \) are applied respectively at the extremities A and B of the rod as shown on the figure.

Given: \( OG = 20 \text{ cm} \).

  1. Calculate the total moment of all forces applied on the rod.
  2. Is the rod at equilibrium? If no, in which direction will it rotate?

Solution - Exercise 1

1) Total Moment:

  • Center of gravity \( G \) is at the middle of the rod. So, \( AG = 0.5 \text{ m} \).
  • Distance \( AO = AG + GO = 0.5 + 0.2 = 0.7 \text{ m} \).
  • Distance \( OB = AB - AO = 1.0 - 0.7 = 0.3 \text{ m} \).
  • Let's define counter-clockwise (CCW) as the positive rotation direction.
  • Moment of Weight (\( \vec{W} \)): Acts at \( G \) (left of \( O \)), pushing down causes CCW rotation.
    \( M(\vec{W}) = + W \times OG = 10 \times 0.2 = +2.0 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)
  • Moment of \( \vec{F}_1 \): Acts at \( B \) (right of \( O \)), pushing down causes CW rotation.
    \( M(\vec{F}_1) = - F_1 \times OB = -2 \times 0.3 = -0.6 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)
  • Moment of \( \vec{F}_2 \): Acts at \( A \) (left of \( O \)). Its vertical component is \( F_2 \sin(60^\circ) \) pointing up, causing CW rotation.
    \( M(\vec{F}_2) = - (F_2 \sin 60^\circ) \times AO = -3 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \times 0.7 \approx -1.819 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)
  • Total Moment (\( \sum M \)): \( +2.0 - 0.6 - 1.819 = \mathbf{-0.419 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}} \)

2) Equilibrium:

Since the sum of the moments is not zero (\( \sum M \neq 0 \)), the rod is not at equilibrium. Because the net moment is negative, it will rotate in the clockwise direction.

Exercise 2

The painter of mass \( m_1 = 82 \text{ kg} \) shown in the adjacent figure is working between two parallel walls on a stand where \( AD = 2.8 \text{ m} \) and of mass \( m_2 = 24 \text{ kg} \). The painter is standing at a point B so that \( AB = 1 \text{ m} \). The stand is hinged horizontally to one wall by a horizontal axis at A while the other end is connected to a cable taken massless and fixed to a ring \( R_2 \) on the other wall.

The paint bucket has a mass \( m_3 = 16 \text{ kg} \) and is placed at a point C on the stand so that \( AC = 2 \text{ m} \). Take \( g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2 \).

  1. Draw a diagram showing the forces acting on the stand.
  2. Determine the magnitude of the tension \( \vec{T} \) exerted by the cable at D.
  3. Determine the magnitude and the direction of the reaction of support \( \vec{R} \) exerted by the axis at A.
  4. Knowing that this cable can withstand a maximum tension of \( T_{\text{max}} = 1392 \text{ N} \). Determine the maximum distance from A at which the painter can stand before cutting the cable.

Solution - Exercise 2

1) Forces Diagram:

Forces acting on stand AD: Reaction \( \vec{R} \) at A, Weight of painter \( \vec{W}_1 \) at B (down), Weight of stand \( \vec{W}_2 \) at its center (down), Weight of bucket \( \vec{W}_3 \) at C (down), and Tension \( \vec{T} \) at D (up-right at \( 30^\circ \)).

2) Magnitude of Tension \( \vec{T} \):

Apply rotational equilibrium about the hinge A (\( \sum M_A = 0 \)):

Center of gravity of stand is at \( AD/2 = 1.4 \text{ m} \) from A.

\( M(\vec{T}) = M(\vec{W}_1) + M(\vec{W}_2) + M(\vec{W}_3) \)

\( (T \sin 30^\circ) \times AD = (m_1 g \times AB) + (m_2 g \times 1.4) + (m_3 g \times AC) \)

\( (T \times 0.5) \times 2.8 = (820 \times 1) + (240 \times 1.4) + (160 \times 2) \)

\( 1.4 T = 820 + 336 + 320 = 1476 \)

\( \mathbf{T = 1054.29 \text{ N}} \)

3) Reaction \( \vec{R} \) at A:

Apply translational equilibrium (\( \sum \vec{F} = 0 \)):

X-axis: \( R_x - T \cos(30^\circ) = 0 \implies R_x = 1054.29 \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 913.04 \text{ N} \)

Y-axis: \( R_y + T \sin(30^\circ) - W_{\text{total}} = 0 \)

\( R_y = (820 + 240 + 160) - (1054.29 \times 0.5) = 1476 - 527.14 = 692.85 \text{ N} \)

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{R_x^2 + R_y^2} = \sqrt{913.04^2 + 692.85^2} \approx \mathbf{1146.16 \text{ N}} \)

Direction: \( \tan \theta = \frac{R_y}{R_x} = \frac{692.85}{913.04} \approx 0.76 \implies \theta \approx \mathbf{37.19^\circ} \) above the horizontal.

4) Maximum distance for painter:

Let \( x \) be the new distance AB. Use \( T_{\text{max}} = 1392 \text{ N} \):

\( (1392 \sin 30^\circ) \times 2.8 = 820(x) + 240(1.4) + 160(2) \)

\( (696) \times 2.8 = 820x + 336 + 320 \)

\( 1948.8 = 820x + 656 \implies 1292.8 = 820x \)

\( \mathbf{x \approx 1.58 \text{ m}} \)

Exercise 3

A system of wheels of different radii are bonded together and rotate in the vertical plane about a common horizontal frictionless axle passing through their common center \( O \). Three forces are acting on the wheels as shown in the adjacent figure.

The radii of small wheel and large wheel are \( R_1 = 20 \text{ cm} \) and \( R_2 = 40 \text{ cm} \) respectively. The moment of inertia of the system about the rotation axle is \( I = 0.2 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m}^2 \).
Given that: \( F_1 = 6 \text{ N} \), \( F_2 = 9 \text{ N} \), \( F_3 = 4 \text{ N} \), \( \sin(\alpha) = 0.94 \).
The system starts from rest at \( t_0 = 0 \).

  1. Apply Newton's 2nd law for rotation to determine the angular acceleration of the wheels. Indicate the sense of rotation.
  2. Determine, at \( t = 10 \text{ s} \), the angular speed of the wheel and the number of turns accomplished.
  3. At \( t = 10 \text{ s} \), a braking force \( \vec{F}_b \) is applied tangentially to the large wheel so that the wheels decelerate uniformly and stop after making \( 10 \) turns.
    • 3.1) Determine the new angular acceleration.
    • 3.2) Deduce the time needed by the wheels to stop during this phase of motion.
    • 3.3) Determine the magnitude of braking force \( F_b \).

Solution - Exercise 3

1) Newton's 2nd Law for Rotation:

Let's find the net torque (\( \sum \tau \)). Counter-clockwise (CCW) is positive.

\( \tau_1 = - F_1 \times R_2 \) (pulls outer wheel CW) \( = -6 \times 0.4 = -2.4 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)

\( \tau_2 = + F_2 \times R_1 \) (pulls inner wheel CCW) \( = +9 \times 0.2 = +1.8 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)

\( \tau_3 = + F_3 \times R_1 \times \sin(\alpha) \) (originates on inner circle, pushes CCW) \( = +4 \times 0.2 \times 0.94 = +0.752 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)

\( \sum \tau = -2.4 + 1.8 + 0.752 = 0.152 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} \)

\( \sum \tau = I \times \theta'' \implies 0.152 = 0.2 \times \theta'' \implies \mathbf{\theta'' = 0.76 \text{ rad/s}^2} \)

Since \( \theta'' > 0 \), the sense of rotation is Counter-Clockwise (positive direction).

2) Angular Speed & Turns at t = 10s:

\( \omega = \omega_0 + \theta'' t = 0 + (0.76)(10) = \mathbf{7.6 \text{ rad/s}} \)

Angular displacement \( \Delta\theta = \frac{1}{2} \theta'' t^2 = 0.5 \times 0.76 \times (10)^2 = 38 \text{ rad} \)

Number of turns \( N = \frac{\Delta\theta}{2\pi} = \frac{38}{2\pi} \approx \mathbf{6.05 \text{ turns}} \)

3) Braking Phase:

3.1) New angular acceleration:
Stops in 10 turns \( \implies \Delta\theta' = 10 \times 2\pi = 20\pi \text{ rad} \).
\( \omega_f^2 - \omega_i^2 = 2 \alpha' \Delta\theta' \implies 0 - (7.6)^2 = 2 \alpha' (20\pi) \)
\( \alpha' = \frac{-57.76}{40\pi} \approx \mathbf{-0.46 \text{ rad/s}^2} \)

3.2) Time needed to stop:
\( \omega_f = \omega_i + \alpha' t' \implies 0 = 7.6 - 0.46 t' \)
\( t' = \frac{7.6}{0.46} \approx \mathbf{16.52 \text{ s}} \)

3.3) Magnitude of braking force \( F_b \):
Apply Newton's 2nd law again with the new torque from \( \vec{F}_b \) applied to the large wheel (radius \( R_2 \)), opposing the motion.
\( \sum \tau_{\text{new}} = I \alpha' \implies (\sum \tau_{\text{old}}) - F_b R_2 = I \alpha' \)
\( 0.152 - F_b(0.4) = 0.2 \times (-0.46) \)
\( 0.152 - 0.4 F_b = -0.092 \)
\( 0.4 F_b = 0.244 \implies \mathbf{F_b = 0.61 \text{ N}} \)
