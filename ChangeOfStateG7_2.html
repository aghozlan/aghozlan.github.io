What happens when we stop heating?

In Part 1 we heated water from ice to steam. Now we ask the reverse question — what happens when we let things cool down, or never heat them at all? Every object left alone will eventually reach the temperature of the room around it.

🌡️
Room Temperature Rule
Any object left without heating or cooling will eventually reach the temperature of its surroundings. This is called thermal equilibrium. In Shatila camp, room temperature is about 20°C in most seasons.
📉
The Cooling Curve
A cooling curve is a temperature-vs-time graph that shows what happens when a substance loses heat. It is the mirror image of the heating curve — same plateaus, same transition temperatures, but going downward.
Q1 1 pt Obj 6 A cup of hot tea at 75°C is left on the table in a room at 22°C. After many hours with no heating, what temperature will the tea reach?
A0°C — it will freeze eventually
B75°C — it keeps the temperature it started with
C22°C — it equilibrates with the room
D50°C — halfway between start and room temperature
🟡 Hint 1: Think about what "no heating" means for a system.
🟣 Hint 2: Objects always exchange heat with the environment until they match it.
🔵 Hint 3: Thermal equilibrium = object temperature = room temperature.

🧪 Bonus Challenge — The Mixture Moment 2 pts
A pure substance is at exactly its melting point. Both solid AND liquid are present at the same time. This is a mixture of states. The temperature stays fixed until all of one state converts to the other.
