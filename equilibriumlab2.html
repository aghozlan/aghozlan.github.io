Part 2 · The Critical Distinction

Same equation.
Different physics.

Equilibrium:  F⃗₁ = −F⃗₂  — two forces on one body, result: rest.
Interaction:  F⃗AB = −F⃗BA  — forces on two different bodies, always paired.

The algebra is identical. The physics context is completely different.
Your mission: master this distinction so no question can fool you.

E2 · The Core Distinction

Equilibrium vs Interaction

Both use the same vector equation. The critical difference: which bodies do the forces act on?

Criterion ⚖ Equilibrium Condition 🔄 Principle of Interaction
Equation F⃗₁ = −F⃗₂ F⃗AB = −F⃗BA
BodiesONE body — both forces on the same objectTWO bodies — each force on a different object
EffectZero net force → object at rest or constant velocityAlways exist in pairs — one cannot exist without the other
Can cancel?✓ Yes — they cancel → equilibrium✗ No — different bodies → cannot cancel each other
Classic trapW⃗ and N⃗ on a book are an EQUILIBRIUM pair (both on book) — NOT an interaction pair. The interaction partner of N⃗ is the force the book exerts on the table, which acts on the TABLE.
✓ The Two-Question Test
Q1: Are both forces on the same body?
→ YES → could be an equilibrium pair

Q2: Do the forces act on different bodies and always coexist?
→ YES → it is an interaction pair
⚠ Most Common Misconception
Student says: "N⃗ and W⃗ on a book are an interaction pair because N⃗ = −W⃗."

WRONG. Both N⃗ and W⃗ act on the book → equilibrium pair.

The true interaction pair: F⃗(table→book) ↑ on book  ↔  F⃗(book→table) ↓ on table.

Question 1 · Interaction pairs

AThey are not equal in magnitude
BThey act on different bodies — F⃗ on the wall, F⃗′ on the man
CThey act in the same direction
DOne is contact, one is distance force
Question 2 · Equilibrium pair identification
A lamp hangs from a ceiling by a wire. Tension T⃗ acts upward on the lamp. Weight W⃗ acts downward on the lamp. These form:
AAn interaction pair — T⃗ is the reaction to W⃗
BAn equilibrium pair — both act on the lamp, making it stationary
CNeither — they are not related to each other
DAn interaction pair — any equal and opposite forces form an interaction pair
Question 3 · The Classic Trap
A student says: "W⃗ and N⃗ on a book are an interaction pair because N⃗ = −W⃗." What is wrong?
A|N⃗| ≠ |W⃗| so they cannot be paired
BBoth N⃗ and W⃗ act on the book → equilibrium pair. The true interaction partner of N⃗ is F⃗(book→table) which acts on the TABLE
CInteraction pairs must involve only contact forces
DN⃗ and W⃗ are not truly equal and opposite
Question 4 · Deduction
A ball of weight W⃗ = 8 N (downward) floats on water. Using the equilibrium condition, deduce F⃗b (buoyancy). Then identify the interaction partner of F⃗b.
AF⃗b = 8 N downward · interaction partner = W⃗
BF⃗b = 8 N upward · interaction partner = force ball exerts on water downward (8 N)
CF⃗b = 4 N upward · balances half of W⃗
DF⃗b = 8 N upward · interaction partner = W⃗ (weight of ball)
Question 5 · True or False
"An interaction pair can produce equilibrium of a single body."
ATrue — the forces are equal and opposite so they cancel
BFalse — interaction pairs act on different bodies so they can NEVER cancel each other or produce equilibrium of one body
