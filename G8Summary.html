Waves, Electromagnetic Spectrum, Shadows, & Reflection

1. Mechanical Waves

Definition: The propagation of a disturbance through a material medium, transferring energy but not matter.

Types of Waves

Transverse Waves

Particles vibrate perpendicular to propagation.
Examples: Water waves 🌊, Strings 🎸.

Longitudinal Waves

Particles vibrate parallel to propagation.
Examples: Sound waves 🔊, P-type earthquake waves.

Characteristics & Formulas

  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between two consecutive crests or compressions (m).
  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from the rest position.
  • Frequency (f): Cycles per second (Hz).
    f = n / t (n: cycles, t: time)
  • Period (T): Time for one cycle (s).
    T = t / n or T = 1 / f
Example: 20 waves pass a point in 5s.
→ f = 20 / 5 = 4 Hz.
→ T = 5 / 20 = 0.25 s.

Solved Examples: Using v = f × λ

🌊 Water Wave (Missing Speed)

Given: f = 2.5 Hz,   λ = 1.2 m.

Find the wave speed (v):

v = f × λ
v = 2.5 × 1.2
v = 3.0 m/s

🔊 Sound Wave (Missing Frequency)

Given: v = 340 m/s,   λ = 0.85 m.

Find the frequency (f):

v = f × λ → f = v / λ
f = 340 / 0.85
f = 400 Hz

2. Electromagnetic Waves

Definition: Waves formed by oscillating electric and magnetic fields. They do not require a medium and travel through a vacuum at the speed of light (c = 3×108 m/s).

The EM Spectrum

📻
Radio Waves: Telecoms, TV/Radio signals
📱
Microwaves: Radar, Cooking, WiFi/Bluetooth
🌡️
Infrared (IR): Thermal imaging, Night vision, Remotes
👁️
Visible Light: Human vision, Photography
☀️
Ultraviolet (UV): Sunbeds, Forgery detection
🦴
X-Rays: Medical imaging (bones), Security
☢️
Gamma Rays: Cancer radiotherapy, Sterilization

Calculations & Unit Conversions

Essential Prefixes Scale

Tera (T): 1012 → Giga (G): 109 → Mega (M): 106 → kilo (k): 103 → Base (100) ← milli (m): 10-3 ← micro (μ): 10-6 ← nano (n): 10-9 ← pico (p): 10-12

📻 FM Radio (Find Wavelength)

Given: A radio station broadcasts at f = 100 MHz.

Speed of light: c = 3 × 108 m/s.

1. Convert f: 100 MHz = 100 × 106 Hz = 108 Hz
2. Formula: c = λ × f  →  λ = c / f
3. Solve: λ = (3 × 108) / 108
λ = 3 m

🦴 X-Rays (Find Frequency)

Given: An X-ray has a wavelength λ = 1.5 nm.

Speed of light: c = 3 × 108 m/s.

1. Convert λ: 1.5 nm = 1.5 × 10-9 m
2. Formula: c = λ × f  →  f = c / λ
3. Solve: f = (3 × 108) / (1.5 × 10-9)
f = 2 × 1017 Hz

3. Light & Shadows

Rectilinear Propagation: Light travels in straight lines within a homogeneous, transparent medium.

Shadow Formation (2D Drawing)

Occurs when an opaque object blocks light rays.

A. Point Light Source

Produces only a sharp Umbra (total shadow).

B. Extended Light Source

Produces an Umbra (dark) and a Penumbra (partial shadow).

4. Reflection of Light

Definition: The bouncing back of light rays when they strike a boundary, returning into the same medium.

Laws & Conditions

  • Condition for Perfect Reflection: The surface must be highly polished, smooth, and shiny (e.g., a plane mirror).
  • Law 1: The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal line all lie in the same plane.
  • Law 2: Angle of incidence (i) = Angle of reflection (r).
    i = r
Reflection vs Scattering:
• Regular Reflection: Smooth surface, parallel rays reflect parallel.
• Scattering (Diffuse): Rough surface, parallel rays reflect in random directions.

Image Formation (Plane Mirror)

  • Virtual: Formed behind the mirror, cannot be captured on a screen.
  • Upright (Erect): Not upside down.
  • Same size: Image height = Object height.
  • Laterally inverted: Left becomes right (and vice versa).
  • Equidistant: Object distance to mirror = Image distance to mirror.
