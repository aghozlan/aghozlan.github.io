Types of Waves

Transverse Waves

Particles vibrate perpendicular to propagation.
Examples: Water waves 🌊, Strings 🎸.

Longitudinal Waves

Particles vibrate parallel to propagation.
Examples: Sound waves 🔊, P-type earthquake waves.

Characteristics & Formulas

  • Wavelength (λ): Distance between two consecutive crests or compressions (m).
  • Amplitude (A): Maximum displacement from the rest position.
  • Frequency (f): Cycles per second (Hz).
    f = n / t (n: cycles, t: time)
  • Period (T): Time for one cycle (s).
    T = t / n or T = 1 / f
Example: 20 waves pass a point in 5s.
→ f = 20 / 5 = 4 Hz.
→ T = 5 / 20 = 0.25 s.

Solved Examples: Using v = f × λ

🌊 Water Wave (Missing Speed)

Given: f = 2.5 Hz,   λ = 1.2 m.

Find the wave speed (v):

v = f × λ
v = 2.5 × 1.2
v = 3.0 m/s

🔊 Sound Wave (Missing Frequency)

Given: v = 340 m/s,   λ = 0.85 m.

Find the frequency (f):

v = f × λ → f = v / λ
f = 340 / 0.85
f = 400 Hz

Umbra Penumbra

4. Reflection of Light

Definition: The bouncing back of light rays when they strike a boundary, returning into the same medium.

Laws & Conditions

  • Condition for Perfect Reflection: The surface must be highly polished, smooth, and shiny (e.g., a plane mirror).
  • Law 1: The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal line all lie in the same plane.
  • Law 2: Angle of incidence (i) = Angle of reflection (r).
    i = r
Reflection vs Scattering:
• Regular Reflection: Smooth surface, parallel rays reflect parallel.
• Scattering (Diffuse): Rough surface, parallel rays reflect in random directions.

Image Formation (Plane Mirror)

  • Virtual: Formed behind the mirror, cannot be captured on a screen.
  • Upright (Erect): Not upside down.
  • Same size: Image height = Object height.
  • Laterally inverted: Left becomes right (and vice versa).
  • Equidistant: Object distance to mirror = Image distance to mirror.
