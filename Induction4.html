Exercise I Electromagnetic Induction — Sliding Conductor in a Magnetic Field (10 pts)

Two long horizontal conducting rails, separated by a distance ℓ = 40 cm, lie in a horizontal plane. Their left ends are connected by a fixed resistor of resistance R = 8.0 Ω. A uniform magnetic field B⃗, of magnitude B = 0.50 T, is directed vertically downward (perpendicular to the rail plane, into the page when viewed from above).

A conducting rod MN of resistance r = 2.0 Ω rests perpendicularly across the rails and can slide without friction. The rails have negligible resistance. At t = 0, the rod is set in motion with a constant velocity v = 3.0 m·s−1 directed to the right (away from R).

The chosen positive direction for the circuit loop and its corresponding normal vector n⃗ are shown in Figure 1.

Given:
B = 0.50 T ℓ = 0.40 m (rail separation) v = 3.0 m·s−1 (constant) R = 8.0 Ω (external resistor) r = 2.0 Ω (resistance of rod MN) Rails and connections: negligible resistance
Part A — Qualitative Analysis
1. As the rod MN moves to the right, the area of the circuit loop AMNB increases. Using Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, explain why an emf is induced in the circuit. State which physical quantity changes and why. (1 pt)
2. Apply Lenz's law to determine the actual direction of the induced current through resistor R (from A to B, or from B to A). Justify step by step: first state the direction of the existing flux Φ and its change, then determine the direction of the induced field, then the current direction. (1.5 pts)
3. The rod MN acts as a seat of emf. Using the magnetic force F⃗ = qv⃗ × B⃗ acting on positive charge carriers inside the rod, determine which end (M or N) is at higher potential. State the polarity (+/−) of the rod. (1 pt)
Part B — Quantitative Study
4. a. Write the expression for the algebraic magnetic flux Φ(t) through the circuit loop at time t (the loop area at t = 0 is zero), given the normal vector n⃗. Express Φ as a function of B, ℓ, v, and t.

b. Using Faraday's law e = −dΦ/dt, write the expression for the induced emf e. Show that |e| = Bℓv when the velocity is constant. Calculate the algebraic value of e. What does its sign indicate? (2 pts)
5. The rod MN (internal resistance r) acts as a generator of emf e connected to the external resistor R.

a. Write the expression for the steady current I in the circuit and calculate its numerical value.

b. Calculate the voltage UAB across resistor R. Verify this result using Ohm's law for R. (1.5 pts)
6. Energy analysis.

a. The magnetic braking force opposing the rod's motion has magnitude Fbrake = BIℓ. Calculate the mechanical power Pmec delivered by the external agent maintaining constant velocity.

b. Calculate the total electrical power Pelec dissipated in the circuit (in R and r together). What law does the equality Pmec = Pelec illustrate?

c. What fraction of the total electrical power is dissipated in R? Calculate the power PR and Pr separately. (2.5 pts)
7. The rod is now replaced by one with resistance r' = 0 (ideal conductor). Without recalculating in full, state the effect on: e, I, UAB, and Pmec. Justify briefly. (1.5 pts)
