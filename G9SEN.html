Exercise 3: Spring–Mass System — Equilibrium and Hooke’s Law (5 points)
1. Condition of equilibrium The two forces must have the same line of action, opposite directions, and equal magnitudes. 1.0 pt
2. Forces on \((S)\) • Weight \(\vec{W}\): vertical line of action, directed downward.
• Spring tension \(\vec{T}\): vertical line of action, directed upward.		 1.0 pt
3. Weight and Tension \(m = 200 \text{ g} = 0.200 \text{ kg}\).
\(W = m \times g = 0.200 \times 10 = 2 \text{ N}\).
At equilibrium: \(\vec{W} + \vec{T} = \vec{0} \Rightarrow T = W = 2 \text{ N}\).		 1.0 pt
4.a. Elongation \(\Delta L\) \(\Delta L = L - L_0 = 20 - 15 = 5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}\). 0.75 pt
4.b. Stiffness \(k\) Hooke’s Law: \(T = k \times \Delta L \Rightarrow k = \dfrac{T}{\Delta L} = \dfrac{2}{0.05} = 40 \text{ N/m}\). 0.75 pt
5. New mass \(m'\) \(\Delta L' = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.10 \text{ m}\).
\(T' = k \times \Delta L' = 40 \times 0.10 = 4 \text{ N}\).
\(m' = T' / g = 4 / 10 = 0.4 \text{ kg} = \mathbf{400 \text{ g}}\).		 0.5 pt
