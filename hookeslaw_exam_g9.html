MARKING SCHEME — VERSION A — FOR TEACHER USE ONLY
Ex 1(a) [1 mk]: The extension of a spring is directly proportional to the applied force, provided the elastic limit is not exceeded.
Ex 1(b) [1 mk]: F = k × x  |  F — Force — Newton (N)  |  k — Spring constant — N/m  |  x — Extension — metre (m)
Ex 1(c) [2 mks, ½ each]: ① Spring    ② Slotted masses    ③ Ruler (metre stick)    ④ Clamp
Ex 2(a) [2 mks, 1 per row]:
Row 4 (400 g): F = 0.400 × 10 = 4 N  |  k = 4 ÷ 0.08 = 50 N/m
Row 5 (500 g): F = 0.500 × 10 = 5 N  |  k = 5 ÷ 0.10 = 50 N/m
Ex 2(b) [4 mks]:
Points (8,4) and (10,5) correctly plotted — 1 mk each
Best-fit straight line through origin — 1 mk
Slope = ΔF/Δx = 1 N / 2 cm = 0.50 N/cm = 50 N/m ∴ k = 50 N/m — 1 mk
Ex 3 P1 [2 mks]: F = k × x = 50 × 0.08 = 4 N
Ex 3 P2 [2 mks]: k = F ÷ x = 6 ÷ 0.12 = 50 N/m
Ex 3 P3 [2 mks]: x = F ÷ k = 9 ÷ 50 = 0.18 m
Ex 4(a) [2 mks]:
No — the spring does NOT obey Hooke’s Law for all values.
Rows 1–3: k = 50 N/m (constant → Hooke’s Law holds).
Rows 4–5: k drops to 40 then 33 N/m; x increases faster than F → elastic limit exceeded.
Ex 4(b) [1 mk]: The slope represents the spring constant k (stiffness of the spring).
Ex 4(c) [1 mk]: The spring permanently deforms (exceeds its elastic limit) and does not return to its original length.
