Exercise 1: Rotating Coil in a Magnetic Field (10 points)

A flat circular coil consists of $N = 100$ turns, each of area $S = 50 \text{ cm}^2$[cite: 3]. The coil is connected to a resistor of resistance $R = 10 \Omega$[cite: 3]. The total resistance of the coil is $r_c = 2 \Omega$[cite: 3]. The coil is placed in a uniform magnetic field $\vec{B}$ of magnitude $B = 0.2 \text{ T}$[cite: 3]. The coil rotates around a diameter perpendicular to $\vec{B}$ at a constant angular velocity $\omega = 10\pi \text{ rad/s}$[cite: 3].

Figure 1: Coil rotating in a uniform magnetic field.

1) Let $\theta = (\vec{B}, \vec{n})$ be the angle between the magnetic field and the normal to the coil[cite: 3]. At $t = 0$, $\theta = 0$[cite: 3].
a) Express $\theta$ as a function of time $t$[cite: 3].
b) Show that the magnetic flux $\Phi$ through the coil at any time $t$ is: $\Phi(t) = 0.1 \cos(10\pi t)$ (Wb)[cite: 3].
2) Determine the expression of the induced electromotive force $e(t)$[cite: 3]. Calculate its maximum value $E_{max}$[cite: 3].
3) Calculate the instantaneous induced current $i(t)$ in the circuit[cite: 3].
4) The mechanical power required to maintain the rotation is dissipated by the Joule effect in the circuit[cite: 3].
a) Express the instantaneous power $P(t)$ dissipated by the Joule effect[cite: 3].
b) Calculate the average power $P_{avg}$ over one period[cite: 3]. (Recall: Average of $\sin^2(\alpha)$ is $1/2$[cite: 3]).
5) If the rotation speed is doubled, how are the maximum e.m.f and the average power affected? Justify[cite: 3].
Exercise 2: RL Transient Response and Circuit Interruption (10 points)

Figure 2: RL circuit schematic.

Part A: Establishment of the Current

a) Which component is responsible for the delay in the establishment of the current? Justify by mentioning the self-induced e.m.f[cite: 2].

a) Express $U_{max}$ and $\tau$ in terms of the circuit constants[cite: 2].

c) Calculate the value of the current $I_0$ in the steady state[cite: 3].
3) Energy considerations:
a) Calculate the magnetic energy $W_m$ stored in the coil at steady state[cite: 3].
b) Compare the power provided by the generator to the power dissipated by the Joule effect in the whole circuit at steady state[cite: 2]. Conclude[cite: 2].

Part B: Opening of the Circuit

4) The steady state being reached, the switch $K$ is suddenly opened[cite: 3].
a) What happens to the energy stored in the coil?[cite: 3]
