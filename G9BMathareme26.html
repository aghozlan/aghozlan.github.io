Lebanese Brevet Curriculum Evaluation Notes

  • Methodology over Final Answer: Partial grades must be awarded for recognizing correct theorems (e.g., Thales, Pythagoras) even if calculation errors occur later.
  • Exact Values: Unless decimal rounding is explicitly requested, results must be kept as simplified fractions or simplified radicals (e.g., 4√2, not 5.65).
  • Geometric Justification: Properties such as "tangent is perpendicular to radius" or "altitudes intersecting at orthocenter" must be explicitly written out by the student to earn full points.

Part Detailed Working Points

Part IV: Line in a Coordinate System (5.5 pts)

Part Detailed Working Points
1) Plotting: Correct placement of A(4, 2), B(-2, -2), and C(-1, 3) on the orthonormal system. 0.50 pts
2) Verify points on (d): y = 5x + 8
For B: 5(-2) + 8 = -10 + 8 = -2 = yB. (B belongs to d)
For C: 5(-1) + 8 = -5 + 8 = 3 = yC. (C belongs to d)
Drawing: Straight line drawn correctly passing through B and C. 		0.75 pts
3) Equation of (AC):
Slope a = (yC - yA) / (xC - xA) = (3 - 2) / (-1 - 4) = -1/5.
y - yA = a(x - xA) ⇒ y - 2 = -1/5(x - 4) ⇒ y = -1/5x + 14/5 		0.75 pts
4) a) Lengths:
AC = √[(-1 - 4)2 + (3 - 2)2] = √[25 + 1] = √26
BC = √[(-1 - -2)2 + (3 - -2)2] = √[1 + 25] = √26 		0.75 pts
4) b) Show Perpendicularity:
Slope of (AC) = -1/5. Slope of (BC) [line d] = 5.
mAC × mBC = (-1/5) × 5 = -1. Therefore, (AC) ⊥ (BC). 		0.50 pts
4) c) Nature of ABC:
Since AC = BC and angle C = 90°, ABC is a right isosceles triangle at C. 		0.25 pts
5) a) Circle Circumscribed:
Since ABC is right at C, hypotenuse [AB] is the diameter. Center I is the midpoint of [AB].
xI = (4 - 2)/2 = 1. yI = (2 - 2)/2 = 0. I(1, 0).
Radius = AB / 2 = √[(-2-4)2 + (-2-2)2] / 2 = √52 / 2 = 2√13 / 2 = √13. 		0.75 pts
5) b) Belongs to x-axis:
Since the ordinate (y-coordinate) of I is 0, it lies on the x-axis. 		0.25 pts
6) a) Nature of CBDA:
I is midpoint of [AB] and [CD] (by symmetry). Diagonals bisect each other ⇒ parallelogram.
Parallelogram with a right angle (angle C = 90°) ⇒ rectangle.
Rectangle with adjacent equal sides (AC = BC) ⇒ Square. 		0.50 pts
6) b) Coordinates of D:
xI = (xC + xD)/2 ⇒ 1 = (-1 + xD)/2 ⇒ xD = 3.
yI = (yC + yD)/2 ⇒ 0 = (3 + yD)/2 ⇒ yD = -3.
D(3, -3). 		0.50 pts
