E2 Circuit Diagrams — Ammeter & Voltmeter Symbols EXPLORE ▾

Now we convert the real multimeter into circuit diagram symbols. The small black dot next to each symbol represents the COM probe connection point — always present but always smaller than the instrument symbol itself.

Ammeter (A) — series Voltmeter (V) — parallel COM point (●)
🔵 Ammeter in Series: Current passes through the ammeter. It must be in the main wire of the circuit — like a checkpoint the electrons walk through.

🟡 Voltmeter in Parallel: Voltage is measured across a component. The voltmeter connects to both sides of the component, like reading the height difference between two points.

E3 Readings & Rules — When Things Go Wrong EXPLAIN ▾

What does the meter tell you?

Understanding unusual readings is as important as understanding normal ones. A skilled electrician reads the meter and instantly knows what's happening in the circuit.

Ammeter reads 0 A
→ Circuit is open (broken). No current can flow.
Voltmeter reads battery voltage
→ Measured component is open/broken (all voltage drops across it).
Voltmeter reads 0 V
→ Either the component has no resistance, or the voltmeter is short-circuiting it.
Ammeter reads very high
→ Possible short circuit — current takes a path with almost no resistance.
