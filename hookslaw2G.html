HOOKE'S LAW
APPLICATIONS

F = k · x
F = spring force (N)  |  k = spring constant (N/m)  |  x = deformation (m)
x > 0 → pure elongation

You know the equation F = k·x. Now you will apply it to real-world systems involving elongation: measuring scales, trampolines, and archery bows.

In the Lebanese curriculum, the deformation is called x — the elongation of the spring. In this lab, we will focus exclusively on cases where the spring is stretched (x > 0).
