Exercise 1: Energy conversion in Induction (10 points)

Two horizontal, parallel, and long conducting rails are separated by a distance \(L = 25 \, \text{cm}\). They are connected to a resistor of resistance \(R = 2.0 \, \Omega\). A conducting rod \(MN\), of mass \(m = 40 \, \text{g}\) and negligible resistance, is placed perpendicular to the rails. A uniform magnetic field \(\vec{B}\), of magnitude \(B = 0.4 \, \text{T}\), is perpendicular to the plane of the rails and directed upwards. Friction is considered negligible.

Figure 1: Top view of the system.

1) The rod moves with a velocity \(\vec{v} = v\vec{i}\).
a) Calculate the magnetic flux \(\Phi\) through the circuit as a function of the position \(x\) of the rod (where \(x = 0\) at the resistor).
b) State Faraday's law. Deduce the expression of the induced e.m.f \(e\) in terms of \(B, L,\) and \(v\).
c) Using Lenz's law, justify the direction of the induced current in the rod (from \(M\) to \(N\) or \(N\) to \(M\)).
2) An external agent pulls the rod with a constant horizontal force \(\vec{F} = F\vec{i}\) to maintain a constant speed \(v = 8 \, \text{m/s}\).
a) Calculate the magnitude \(I\) of the induced current.
b) Determine the characteristics (direction and magnitude) of the Laplace force \(\vec{f}_L\) acting on the rod.
c) By applying Newton's second law, calculate the magnitude \(F\) of the external force.
3) Consider the energy aspect of the motion.
a) Calculate the mechanical power \(P_{mech}\) provided by the external force.
b) Calculate the electrical power \(P_J\) dissipated by the Joule effect. Compare the two results and conclude.
Exercise 2: Characterization of a Coil (10 points)

To determine the characteristics \((L, r)\) of a coil, a student sets up a circuit comprising a DC voltage source \(E = 6.0 \, \text{V}\), the coil, a resistor \(R = 50 \, \Omega\), and a switch \(G\). An oscilloscope is used to record the voltage \(u_R\) across the resistor.

Figure 2: RL Circuit diagram.

1) At \(t = 0\), the switch \(G\) is closed.
a) Name the phenomenon occurring in the coil. Explain its cause.
b) Draw a schematic showing how to connect the oscilloscope to observe \(u_R\).
2) Using the law of addition of voltages, establish the differential equation for the current \(i(t)\). Show that it can be written as: \(\frac{di}{dt} + \frac{1}{\tau}i = A\), where \(\tau\) and \(A\) are constants to be expressed.
3) The steady-state voltage measured by the oscilloscope is \(U_R = 5.0 \, \text{V}\).
a) Calculate the steady-state current \(I_{max}\).
b) Show that the internal resistance of the coil is \(r = 10 \, \Omega\).
4) The student notices that at \(t = 5 \, \text{ms}\), the voltage \(u_R\) has reached \(63\%\) of its maximum value.
a) What does this time represent? Deduce the value of the inductance \(L\).
b) Express the magnetic energy \(E_m\) stored in the coil at steady state and calculate its value.
5) The switch \(G\) is opened. A neon lamp connected in parallel with the coil flashes briefly. Explain this observation by mentioning the expression of the voltage \(u_L\) across the coil.

QExpected Answer / Distribution of PointsPoints
1.a\(\Phi = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{S} = B \cdot S \cdot \cos(0) = B(L \cdot x)\). Calculation: \(0.4 \cdot 0.25 \cdot x = 0.1x\) (Wb).1.0
1.bFaraday: \(e = -d\Phi/dt\). Since \(x\) changes, \(dx/dt = v\). So, \(e = -BLv\).1.0
1.cInduced flux must oppose increasing flux. Current is directed from \(N\) to \(M\) (Right-hand rule).1.0
2.a\(|e| = B L v = 0.4 \cdot 0.25 \cdot 8 = 0.8 \, \text{V}\). \(I = |e|/R = 0.8/2.0 = 0.4 \, \text{A}\).1.5
2.b\(\vec{f}_L = I(\vec{MN} \times \vec{B})\). Magnitude: \(f_L = BIL = 0.4 \cdot 0.4 \cdot 0.25 = 0.04 \, \text{N}\). Direction: opposite to \(\vec{v}\).1.5
2.cAt const. speed, \(\sum \vec{F} = 0 \implies \vec{F} + \vec{f}_L = 0 \implies F = f_L = 0.04 \, \text{N}\).1.0
3.a\(P_{mech} = F \cdot v = 0.04 \cdot 8 = 0.32 \, \text{W}\).1.5
3.b\(P_J = R I^2 = 2.0 \cdot (0.4)^2 = 0.32 \, \text{W}\). Equality: Mech. energy is entirely converted to heat.1.5
