E3 · Equilibrium Condition

Condition of Equilibrium

A body acted on by two forces is in equilibrium when:

F1 = −F2
Equal magnitudes · Opposite directions · Same line of action
|F₁| = |F₂|
The two forces are equally strong
Opposite directions
One up, one down — or one left, one right
Same line of action
Both forces along the same straight line
Three Examples with Complete Diagrams
Example 1
Book Resting on a Horizontal Table
Forces on the book:
• Weight W — gravitational force, origin: centre of gravity G, direction: vertically downward, type: distance force
• Normal reaction N — from table surface, origin: contact point (bottom face of book), direction: vertically upward, type: contact force
N = −W
N: vertical ↑ from contact point · W: vertical ↓ from G · |N| = |W|
If W = 5 N downward, deduce N:
AN = 5 N, vertically downward
BN = 5 N, vertically upward
CN = 10 N, vertically upward
Example 2
Ball Floating on the Surface of Water
Forces on the ball:
• Weight W — origin: centre of ball G, direction: vertically downward
• Buoyancy Fb — from water, origin: lowest contact point of ball with water, direction: vertically upward
Fb = −W
Fb: vertical ↑ from contact · W: vertical ↓ from G · |Fb| = |W|
Buoyancy is exerted by the water. It is a:
AContact force — water touches the ball directly
BForce at a distance — like gravity
Example 3
Body Suspended from a Spring
Forces on the body:
• Weight W — origin: centre of gravity G, direction: vertically downward
• Tension T — from spring, origin: top contact point of body with spring, direction: vertically upward
T = −W
T: vertical ↑ from contact point · W: vertical ↓ from G · |T| = |W|
If W = 12 N downward, deduce T:
AT = 12 N, vertically downward
BT = 6 N, vertically upward
CT = 12 N, vertically upward
E3 · Principle of Interaction

Principle of Interaction

Statement

When body A exerts a force on body B, at the same moment, body B exerts a force on body A.

FA/B = −FB/A
|FA/B| = |FB/A| — equal magnitudes, opposite directions, different bodies
⚠ Key: These forces act on different bodies — they do NOT cancel each other.
Box / Table: Ftable/box (table pushes box ↑) = −Fbox/table (box pushes table ↓)
Ball / Water: Fwater/ball (water pushes ball ↑) = −Fball/water (ball pushes water ↓)
Body / Spring: Fspring/body (spring pulls body ↑) = −Fbody/spring (body pulls spring ↓)
🎬 Live Demo — Person Pushing a Wall
Each bounce: force decreases as the ball loses energy!
F(ball/floor) — ball pushes floor ↓
F(floor/ball) — floor pushes ball ↑ (reaction)
When the person pushes harder on the wall, the reaction force from the wall:
AStays the same — it doesn't change
BIncreases by the same amount — always equal in magnitude
CDecreases — the wall resists less
