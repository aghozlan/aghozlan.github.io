Objective 1 · Locating Genetic Information

Where does the cell store its secrets?

📍 Location of Genetic Information

Genetic information is located in the nucleus of eukaryotic cells, carried by structures called chromosomes.

🌟 Amazing fact: Every single cell of your body (except red blood cells) contains the complete blueprint for building YOU — all ~3 billion DNA base pairs!

Objective 3 · Definition

What is a Chromosome?

📘 Definition

A chromosome is a thread-like structure composed of DNA tightly coiled around proteins (histones), found in the nucleus. It carries genetic information in the form of genes.

🔬 Key Parts

  • 🯉 Telomeres — protective caps at both ends
  • 🯊 Centromere — pinch point connecting two chromatids
  • 🔵 Short arm (p) and Long arm (q)
  • 🟣 Bands — staining pattern unique to each chromosome

⚡ A chromosome is only visible under a light microscope during cell division, when DNA is maximally condensed.

Objective 4 · Karyotype

What is a Karyotype?

📘 Definition

A karyotype is the complete set of chromosomes in a cell, arranged by pairs, in order of decreasing size, as viewed during cell division (metaphase).

Literally: “karyon” (nucleus) + “typos” (form) → The form of the nucleus

🧪 Medical Uses

Karyotype analysis detects chromosomal disorders such as Down syndrome (trisomy 21), Turner syndrome, and Klinefelter syndrome.

Normal Human Karyotype (Male, 46, XY)

23 pairs · 46 chromosomes total

Objective 5 · Two Types of Chromosomes

Autosomal vs Gonosomal

🧬

Autosomes

  • 📌 Pairs 1–22 (22 pairs = 44 chromosomes)
  • 🔄 Identical in males and females
  • 🎯 Control body (somatic) traits: height, eye colour, blood type…
  • 👥 Both chromosomes in each pair carry the same genes

Chromosome 1 carries genes for blood clotting, cholesterol metabolism, and much more.

⛢️

Gonosomes (Sex Chromosomes)

  • 📌 Pair 23 (1 pair = 2 chromosomes)
  • ♀ Female: XX
  • ♂ Male: XY
  • 🎯 Determine biological sex of the individual
  • 🧬 X carries many genes; Y is smaller, carries fewer

The father contributes either X or Y — this determines the sex!

🤯 Surprise! The X chromosome carries about 900 genes, while the Y carries only about 55 genes — yet the Y is what makes someone biologically male!

Objective 6 · Chromosomal Formula

Writing the Chromosomal Formula

📐 The Formula Structure

2n = A + GS

Total chromosomes = Autosomes + Gonosomes

🧩 Legend

  • 2n — total number of chromosomes (diploid number)
  • A — number of autosomes
  • GS — gonosomes (sex chromosomes: XX or XY)

Objective 7 · Karyotype Criteria

How are Chromosomes Arranged in a Karyotype?

Scientists follow strict international rules — the Denver criteria (1960):

1
Decreasing Size

Arranged from the largest (pair 1) to the smallest (pair 22). Pair 23 is placed separately at the end.

2
Centromere Position
3
Homologous Pairs

Grouped in homologous pairs — one chromosome from mother and one from father, placed side-by-side.

4
Sex Chromosomes Last

The gonosomes (X and Y) are always placed as the last pair, clearly separated from the autosomes.

Centromere Positions — Visual

Metacentric

p = q

Submetacentric

p < q

Acrocentric

p very short
