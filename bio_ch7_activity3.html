Objective 1 · Locating Genetic Information

Where does the cell store its secrets?

📍 Location of Genetic Information

Genetic information is located in the nucleus of eukaryotic cells, carried by structures called chromosomes.

🧪 Also found in…

Small amounts of genetic information also exist in mitochondria (and chloroplasts in plants) — but the main store is always the nucleus.

🌟 Amazing fact: Every single cell of your body (except red blood cells) contains the complete blueprint for building YOU — all ~3 billion DNA base pairs!

Objective 3 · Definition

What is a Chromosome?

📖 Definition

A chromosome is a thread-like structure composed of DNA tightly coiled around proteins (histones), found in the nucleus of cells. It carries the cell's genetic information in the form of genes.

🔬 Key Parts

  • 🟡 Telomeres — protective "caps" at both ends
  • 🟢 Centromere — pinch point connecting two chromatids
  • 🔵 Short arm (p) and Long arm (q)
  • 🟣 Bands — staining pattern unique to each chromosome

⚡ A chromosome is only visible under a light microscope during cell division (mitosis/meiosis), when DNA is maximally condensed.

Objective 4 · Karyotype

What is a Karyotype?

📖 Definition

A karyotype is the complete set of chromosomes in a cell, arranged by pairs, in order of decreasing size, as viewed during cell division (metaphase).

Literally means: "karyon" (nucleus) + "typos" (form) → The form of the nucleus

🔬 How it's made

  1. Blood sample taken from patient
  2. White blood cells grown in lab
  3. Cell division arrested at metaphase
  4. Chromosomes stained and photographed
  5. Pairs arranged, numbered, and analysed

🩺 Medical Uses

Karyotype analysis detects chromosomal disorders such as Down syndrome (trisomy 21), Turner syndrome, and Klinefelter syndrome.

Objective 5 · Two Types of Chromosomes

Autosomal vs Gonosomal

🧬

Autosomes

  • 📌 Pairs 1–22 (22 pairs = 44 chromosomes)
  • 🔄 Identical in males and females
  • 🎯 Control body (somatic) traits: height, eye colour, blood type, etc.
  • 👫 Both chromosomes in each pair have the same genes

Example: chromosome 1 carries genes for blood clotting, cholesterol metabolism, and much more.

⚧️

Gonosomes (Sex Chromosomes)

  • 📌 Pair 23 (1 pair = 2 chromosomes)
  • ♀ Female: XX
  • ♂ Male: XY
  • 🎯 Determine biological sex of the individual
  • 🧬 X carries many genes; Y is smaller, carries fewer genes

The father always contributes either X or Y to the child — this determines the sex!

🤯 Surprise! The X chromosome carries about 900 genes, while the Y chromosome carries only about 55 genes — yet the Y is what makes someone biologically male!

Objective 6 · Chromosomal Formula

Writing the Chromosomal Formula

📐 The Formula Structure

2n = A + GS

Total chromosomes = Autosomes + Gonosomes

🧩 Legend

  • 2n = total number of chromosomes (diploid)
  • A = number of autosomes
  • GS = gonosomes (sex chromosomes)

♀ Human Female

2n = 44A + XX

44 autosomes + 2 X chromosomes = 46 total

♂ Human Male

2n = 44A + XY

44 autosomes + X + Y chromosomes = 46 total

Objective 7 · Karyotype Criteria

How are Chromosomes Arranged in a Karyotype?

Scientists follow strict international rules to arrange chromosomes. These are the Denver criteria (established 1960):

1
Decreasing Size

Chromosomes are arranged from the largest (pair 1) to the smallest (pair 22). Pair 23 (sex chromosomes) is placed separately at the end.

2
Centromere Position

Within size groups, chromosomes are ordered by position of the centromere:
• Metacentric — centromere in middle (p ≈ q)
• Submetacentric — centromere slightly off-centre (p < q)
• Acrocentric — centromere near one end (p very short)

3
Banding Pattern

Each chromosome has a unique G-band pattern (dark and light bands after Giemsa staining), used to identify individual chromosomes precisely.

4
Homologous Pairs

Chromosomes are grouped in homologous pairs — two chromosomes carrying the same genes (one from mother, one from father) placed side-by-side.

5
Sex Chromosomes Last

The gonosomes (X and Y) are always placed as the last pair, regardless of their size, clearly separated from the autosomes.

Objective 8 · Compare Karyotypes

Male ♂ vs Female ♀ Karyotype

♀ Female Karyotype

2n = 44A + XX
  • ✅ 46 chromosomes total
  • ✅ 22 pairs of autosomes
  • ✅ Two identical X chromosomes
  • ✅ Both X chromosomes similar in size
  • ✅ No Y chromosome

♂ Male Karyotype

2n = 44A + XY
  • ✅ 46 chromosomes total
  • ✅ 22 pairs of autosomes
  • ✅ One X chromosome (large)
  • ✅ One Y chromosome (small)
  • ⚡ X ≠ Y in size and gene content

🔬 Key difference: Only the sex chromosomes differ between male and female karyotypes. All 44 autosomes are identical in both sexes!

Click & Reveal — Do You Know?

Click each card to reveal the answer. Test yourself first!

What is a karyotype?

The complete set of chromosomes in a cell, arranged in homologous pairs by decreasing size.

How many chromosome pairs do humans have?

23 pairs — 22 pairs of autosomes + 1 pair of gonosomes (sex chromosomes).

What are gonosomes?

The sex chromosomes (pair 23). XX in females, XY in males. They determine biological sex.

Write the chromosomal formula for a normal human female.

2n = 44A + XX (46 chromosomes total)

What is the centromere?

The constriction point on a chromosome that connects the two chromatids. Used to classify chromosome type.

What is trisomy 21?

An extra copy of chromosome 21 (3 instead of 2), causing Down syndrome. Formula: 2n = 47.
