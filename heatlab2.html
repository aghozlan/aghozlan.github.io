E2 · Concepts

Heat vs Temperature

Two completely different quantities — learn them both!

🔥
HEAT (Q)
Heat is a form of energy that flows from a hot object to a cold one. It depends on the number of particles AND their speed.
Unit: Joule (J)
🌡️
TEMPERATURE (T)
Temperature measures how fast the particles of an object are moving on average. Higher temperature = faster particles.
Unit: degree Celsius (°C)
🔑 Key Difference
• Heat = total energy → depends on how much material there is
• Temperature = average particle energy → does NOT depend on amount
• A small spark and a large fire can reach the same temperature, but the fire has FAR more heat energy.

Heat travels through a solid material by passing energy from particle to particle — like dominoes falling! Watch the animation below.

E3 · Heat Transfer

🌊 Convection

Heat travels through liquids and gases by the actual movement of the fluid itself — hot fluid rises, cool fluid sinks, creating a current!

CONVECTION — Fluid Current
Click to heat the bottom
What happens:
🔴 Hot fluid at the bottom expands → becomes lighter → rises
🔵 Cool fluid at the top contracts → becomes heavier → sinks
🌀 This creates a convection current — a circular loop of moving fluid
🏠 Examples: boiling water in a pot, sea breezes, heating a room with a radiator
Convection Question

AIt becomes heavier so it floats up
BIt expands and becomes less dense (lighter) so it rises
CHeat always moves upward
E3 · Heat Transfer

☀️ Radiation

Heat travels as invisible waves — no material needed at all! This is how the Sun heats the Earth across empty space.

RADIATION — Heat Waves
Click to start radiation
Key facts:
☀️ Radiation travels as infrared waves — a type of electromagnetic wave
🚀 It can travel through empty space (vacuum) — no particles needed
⚫ Dark, rough surfaces absorb AND emit radiation better
⚪ Shiny, light surfaces reflect radiation — they absorb and emit less
🏠 Examples: Sun heating Earth, sitting near a fire, microwave oven
Radiation Question

AShiny silver surface
BWhite smooth surface
CDull black surface
