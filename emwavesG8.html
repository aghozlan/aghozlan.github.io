E3 · Radio Waves

📻 Radio Waves

Physics Properties
Wavelength1 mm → 100 km
Frequency3 Hz → 300 GHz
EnergyLowest of all EM
PenetrationPasses through walls, skin
SourceAntennas, lightning, stars
🤖 AI Era Use
AI-powered radio telescopes like the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) use machine learning to process petabytes of radio wave data from deep space — detecting patterns humans could never find manually. Radio waves also power 5G networks where AI optimises signal routing in real time for billions of devices.
Real-World Applications
📻
AM/FM Radio
Broadcasting music & news
📡
Satellite TV
Signals from space to dish
📱
Mobile Networks
4G, 5G, WiFi calls
🔭
Radio Astronomy
Mapping galaxies
💼 Career Path
Telecommunications Engineer — designs and optimises wireless networks. Average salary: $85,000/year. Requires physics + computer science. Companies: Nokia, Ericsson, Huawei, Apple.
Did You Know?
The first image ever of a black hole (M87*, 2019) was captured using radio waves — not visible light. The Event Horizon Telescope linked radio telescopes across 8 countries to create a virtual telescope the size of Earth. The data was so large it had to be physically flown on hard drives to processing centres because the internet was too slow.
E3 · Microwaves

📡 Microwaves

Physics Properties
Wavelength1 mm → 1 m
Frequency300 MHz → 300 GHz
EnergyLow — heats water molecules
PenetrationPenetrates food, clouds, rain
SourceMagnetron tubes, satellites
🤖 AI Era Use
Autonomous vehicles use microwave radar (RADAR = Radio Detection And Ranging) to detect objects in fog, rain, and darkness where cameras fail. Tesla, Waymo and other self-driving AI systems fuse radar data with camera data to make split-second decisions. Also: weather prediction AI uses microwave satellite data to model global weather patterns.
Real-World Applications

Vibrates water molecules → heat
🛰️

Location accuracy to 1 meter
🌦️
Weather Radar
Tracking storms, rainfall
🚗
Speed Cameras
Doppler radar detects speed
💼 Career Path
Radar Systems Engineer — designs radar for aviation, weather, defence. Companies: Raytheon, Thales, Airbus Defence. Lebanon connection: radar stations monitor all aircraft entering Lebanese airspace.
E3 · Infrared

🌡️ Infrared Radiation

Physics Properties
Wavelength700 nm → 1 mm
Frequency300 GHz → 430 THz
EnergyFelt as heat by skin
PenetrationBlocked by glass, absorbed by skin
SourceAll warm objects emit IR
🤖 AI Era Use
Face ID on your iPhone uses an infrared dot projector — 30,000 invisible IR dots map your face in 3D. An AI neural network then matches the pattern to unlock. Also: medical AI analyses infrared thermal images to detect cancer tumours (which are warmer than surrounding tissue) and COVID fever screening used IR cameras at airports.

E3 · Gamma Rays

☢️ Gamma Rays

Physics Properties
WavelengthLess than 0.01 nm
FrequencyAbove 30 EHz
EnergyHighest of all EM — extremely ionising
PenetrationPenetrates everything — only thick lead/concrete blocks
SourceRadioactive decay, nuclear reactions, neutron stars
🤖 AI Era Use
Gamma-ray astronomy AI — NASA's Fermi telescope detects gamma-ray bursts (the most energetic explosions in the universe). AI automatically classifies and alerts scientists within seconds. Cancer treatment AI — Gamma Knife radiosurgery uses AI to precisely aim 192 gamma beams at brain tumours with sub-millimetre accuracy, sparing healthy tissue.

BVisible light — submarines have windows

DMicrowaves — they penetrate food so they penetrate water
