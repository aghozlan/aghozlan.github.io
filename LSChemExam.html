Part C — Percentage Yield  (4 points)

A student performs Reaction B3 by mixing 0.20 mol of ethanoic acid (CH3COOH) with an excess of methanol (CH3OH). After heating under reflux and purification, the student collects 11.84 g of the pure ester.

Given:  M(C) = 12  |  M(H) = 1  |  M(O) = 16   (g mol−1)

EXERCISE 2  —  Aqueous Solutions: Strong Acid and Strong Base 10 pts

Solution S1 of hydrochloric acid (HCl) has volume V1 = 200 mL and pH = 2.0.  Solution S2 of sodium hydroxide (NaOH) is prepared by dissolving 0.08 g of pure NaOH in a total volume V2 = 100 mL.

Given at 25 °C:
  • Molar masses: M(Na) = 23, M(O) = 16, M(H) = 1   g mol−1
  • Kw = [H3O+][OH−] = 1.0 × 10−14
  • HCl and NaOH are strong (fully dissociated) electrolytes.
  • log 2 ≈ 0.30
1 pt 1.  Write the dissociation equation of HCl in water. Deduce [HCl] = [H3O+] in S1.
2 pts 2.  Calculate [NaOH] in S2, then deduce [OH−] and [H3O+] in S2.
1.5 pts 3.  Calculate the pH of S2. Is S2 acidic, neutral, or basic? Justify.
4 pts 4.  VA = 50 mL of S1 is mixed with VB = 50 mL of S2 to form mixture M.
1 pt 4.a.  Write the ionic equation for the neutralisation reaction.
1.5 pts 4.b.  Calculate the moles of H3O+ and OH− before mixing. Identify the species in excess after reaction.
1.5 pts 4.c.  Calculate the pH of mixture M. Is M acidic, neutral, or basic?
1.5 pts 5.  What volume VB of S2 must be added to 50 mL of S1 to produce a neutral solution (pH = 7)? Show all reasoning.

CH3—CH2—COOH

CH3CH2CH2OH + 2[O] → CH3CH2COOH + H2O

Part C — Percentage Yield  (4 pts)

0.5 pt C1. Molecular formula of methyl ethanoate (methyl acetate)
C3H6O2
Derived from CH3COOCH3: C×3, H×6, O×2.
1.5 pts C2. Theoretical mass
  • Molar mass of C3H6O2 = 3(12) + 6(1) + 2(16) = 36 + 6 + 32 = 74 g mol−1  (0.5 pt)
  • Mole ratio acid : ester = 1 : 1, so n(ester) theoretical = n(acid) = 0.20 mol  (0.5 pt)
  • Theoretical mass = 0.20 × 74 = 14.8 g  (0.5 pt)
2 pts C3. Percentage yield and explanation
  • % yield = (actual mass / theoretical mass) × 100  (0.5 pt for correct formula)
  • % yield = (11.84 / 14.8) × 100 = 80 %  (1 pt)
  • Explanation (0.5 pt — any one valid reason):
    • Esterification is a reversible equilibrium; equilibrium is reached before complete conversion.
    • Losses during separation / purification / transfer of product.

EXERCISE 2 — Aqueous Solutions 10 pts

1 pt Q1. Dissociation of HCl and [HCl] of S1
HCl + H2O → H3O+ + Cl−
[H3O+] = 10−pH = 10−2 = 0.01 mol L−1
Since HCl is strong: [HCl] = [H3O+] = 10−2 mol L−1
0.5 pt equation + 0.5 pt concentration. ECF applies if pH misread.
2 pts Q2. Concentration and ion concentrations of S2
  • M(NaOH) = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40 g mol−1
  • n(NaOH) = 0.08 / 40 = 2.0 × 10−3 mol  (0.5 pt)
  • [NaOH] = n/V = 2.0 × 10−3 / 0.100 = 2.0 × 10−2 mol L−1  (0.5 pt)
  • NaOH → Na+ + OH−  ⟹  [OH−] = 2.0 × 10−2 mol L−1  (0.5 pt)
  • [H3O+] = Kw / [OH−] = 10−14 / (2.0 × 10−2) = 5.0 × 10−13 mol L−1  (0.5 pt)
1.5 pts Q3. pH of S2 and characterisation
  • pH = −log[H3O+] = −log(5.0 × 10−13)  (0.5 pt for method)
  • = 13 − log 5 = 13 − (1 − log 2) = 13 − (1 − 0.30) = 13 − 0.70
  • pH(S2) = 12.30  (0.5 pt)
  • pH > 7 → S2 is basic  (0.5 pt)
ECF: if [H₃O⁺] from Q2 is wrong but correctly used here, award method marks.
4 pts total Q4. Mixing 50 mL S1 + 50 mL S2
1 pt 4.a. Ionic neutralisation equation
H3O+ + OH− → 2 H2O
Must show ionic form. Do not accept molecular form alone.
1.5 pts 4.b. Moles before mixing — excess species
  • n(H3O+) = 0.050 × 10−2 = 5.0 × 10−4 mol  (0.5 pt)
  • n(OH−) = 0.050 × 2.0 × 10−2 = 1.0 × 10−3 mol  (0.5 pt)
  • n(OH−) > n(H3O+) → OH− is in excess  (0.5 pt)
  • n(OH−)excess = 1.0 × 10−3 − 5.0 × 10−4 = 5.0 × 10−4 mol
1.5 pts 4.c. pH of mixture M
  • Vtotal = 50 + 50 = 100 mL = 0.100 L
  • [OH−]M = 5.0 × 10−4 / 0.100 = 5.0 × 10−3 mol L−1  (0.5 pt)
  • [H3O+]M = 10−14 / (5.0 × 10−3) = 2.0 × 10−12 mol L−1  (0.5 pt)
  • pH = −log(2.0 × 10−12) = 12 − log 2 = 12 − 0.30 = 11.70  (0.5 pt)
  • pH > 7 → mixture M is basic
1.5 pts Q5. Volume of S2 needed for neutralisation (pH = 7)
  • At equivalence: n(H3O+) = n(OH−)  (0.5 pt for condition)
  • n(H3O+) in 50 mL S1 = 5.0 × 10−4 mol (from Q4)
  • V(S2) = n(OH−) / [NaOH] = 5.0 × 10−4 / (2.0 × 10−2)  (0.5 pt)
  • V(S2) = 0.025 L = 25 mL  (0.5 pt)
ECF from Q1 and Q2. Award full marks if method is correct with ECF values.
