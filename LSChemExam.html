Grade 12 Life Sciences (LS) — Chemistry Mid-Term Exam

Class: 11S  |  Time Allowed: 60 minutes  |  Total Marks: 24

EXERCISE 1: Organic Chemistry — Structures, Reactivity, and Yield (14 points)

Part A: IUPAC Nomenclature (4 points)

Give the correct IUPAC name for each of the following chemical compounds:

1. CH3—CH(CH3)—CH2—CHO
2. CH3—CH2—CO—CH3
3. CH3—CH2—CH(OH)—CH3
4. HCOOH

Part B: Organic Reactions and Percentage Yield (10 points)

Copy and complete the following chemical equations. Show the structural formulas for the major organic products formed, and state the specific type of reaction taking place.

1. CH3—CH2—CH2—OH  → [ KMnO4 (excess) / H+ ]  → ...
2. CH3—COOCH2CH3 + H2O  → [ H+ ]  ←→ ...
3. CH3—COOH + CH3—OH  → [ H2SO4, heat ]  ←→ ...

Experimental Application Question:

A student performs Reaction 3 in the laboratory by mixing 0.20 mol of ethanoic acid (CH3—COOH) with an excess of methanol (CH3—OH). After heating the mixture under reflux and separating the products, the student collects 11.84 g of the pure liquid ester.

3.a. Write the molecular formula of the ester produced.
3.b. Calculate the theoretical mass of the ester that should be produced if the reaction went to completion.
3.c. Calculate the percentage yield of this esterification reaction.

EXERCISE 2: Aqueous Solutions — Strong Acid and Strong Base (10 points)

An aqueous solution S1 of hydrochloric acid (HCl) has V1 = 200 mL and pH = 2.0. Solution S2 (NaOH) is prepared by dissolving 0.08 g of pure NaOH in volume V2 = 100 mL.

Given constants and data at 25°C:
  • Molar masses: M(Na)=23, M(O)=16, M(H)=1 g/mol
  • Water ion-product constant: Kw = 1.0 × 10-14
