Grade 12 Life Sciences (LS) — Chemistry Mid-Term Exam

Time Allowed: 60 minutes  |  Total Marks: 24 points

EXERCISE 1: Organic Chemistry — Structures, Reactivity, and Yield (14 points)

Part A: IUPAC Nomenclature (4 points)

Give the correct IUPAC name for each of the following chemical compounds:

1. CH₃—CH(CH₃)—CH₂—CHO
2. CH₃—CH₂—CO—CH₃
3. CH₃—CH₂—CH(OH)—CH₃
4. HCOOH

Part B: Organic Reactions and Percentage Yield (10 points)

Copy and complete the following chemical equations. Show the structural formulas for the major organic products formed, and state the specific type of reaction taking place.

1. CH₃—CH₂—CH₂—OH  → [ KMnO₄ (excess) / H⁺ ]  → ...
2. CH₃—COOCH₂CH₃ + H₂O  → [ H⁺ ]  ←→ ...
3. CH₃—COOH + CH₃—OH  → [ H₂SO₄, heat ]  ←→ ...

Experimental Application Question:

A student performs Reaction 3 in the laboratory by mixing 0.20 mol of ethanoic acid (CH₃—COOH) with an excess of methanol (CH₃—OH). After heating the mixture under reflux and separating the products, the student collects 11.84 g of the pure liquid ester.

3.a. Write the molecular formula of the ester produced.
3.b. Calculate the theoretical mass of the ester that should be produced if the reaction went to completion.
(Given: M(C) = 12 g/mol, M(O) = 16 g/mol, M(H) = 1 g/mol)
3.c. Calculate the percentage yield of this esterification reaction.

EXERCISE 2: Aqueous Solutions — Strong Acid and Strong Base (10 points)

An aqueous solution S₁ of hydrochloric acid (HCl, a strong acid) has a volume V₁ = 200 mL and a pH of 2.0. Another aqueous solution S₂ of sodium hydroxide (NaOH, a strong base) is prepared by dissolving 0.08 g of pure NaOH pellets in enough distilled water to make a final volume V₂ = 100 mL.

Given constants and data at 25°C:
  • Molar masses: M(Na) = 23 g/mol, M(O) = 16 g/mol, M(H) = 1 g/mol
  • Water ion-product constant: K_w = 1.0 × 10⁻¹⁴
1. Calculate the molar concentration of hydronium ions [H₃O⁺] in solution S₁.
2. Calculate the molar concentration of solution S₂, and deduce its pH at 25°C.
3. The two solutions S₁ and S₂ are mixed together in a beaker:
• Write the net ionic equation for the reaction that occurs.
• Determine whether the final mixture is acidic, basic, or neutral. Justify your answer by calculating the remaining excess moles of [H₃O⁺] or [OH⁻].

Teacher's Answer Key & Marking Guide

For Evaluation and Grading Reference

Exercise 1 Solution Key

Part A: Nomenclature

1. 3-methylbutanal (Aldehyde functional group) [1 point]
2. Butan-2-one or 2-butanone (Ketone functional group) [1 point]
3. Butan-2-ol or 2-butanol (Secondary alcohol functional group) [1 point]
4. Methanoic acid (Carboxylic acid functional group) [1 point]

Part B: Reactions & Yield

1. Major Product: CH₃—CH₂—COOH (Propanoic acid)
Reaction Type: Mild oxidation (Primary alcohol oxidized to carboxylic acid via strong oxidant) [2 points]
2. Major Products: CH₃—COOH + CH₃CH₂—OH (Ethanoic acid + Ethanol)
Reaction Type: Ester hydrolysis (Acid-catalyzed reversible ester cleavage) [2 points]
3. Major Product: CH₃—COOCH₃ + H₂O (Methyl ethanoate + water)
Reaction Type: Esterification / Condensation reaction [2 points]

Experimental Application & Yield Breakdown

3.a. Molecular Formula: C₃H₆O₂ (Methyl ethanoate) [1 point]
3.b. Theoretical Mass Calculation:
• Molar mass of C₃H₆O₂ = (3 × 12) + (6 × 1) + (2 × 16) = 74 g/mol.
• Stoichiometric ratio is 1:1; limiting reactant ethanoic acid = 0.20 mol.
• Theoretical Moles of Ester = 0.20 mol.
• Theoretical Mass = 0.20 mol × 74 g/mol = 14.80 g [1.5 points]
3.c. Percentage Yield Calculation:
• Percentage Yield = (Actual Mass / Theoretical Mass) × 100
• Yield = (11.84 g / 14.80 g) × 100 = 80% [1.5 points]

Exercise 2 Solution Key

1. Concentration of S₁:
Hydrochloric acid is a strong completely dissociated acid, so:
[H₃O⁺] = 10^(-pH) = 10^(-2.0) = 1.0 × 10⁻² mol/L [2 points]
2. Concentration and pH of S₂:
• Molar mass of NaOH = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40 g/mol.
• Moles of dissolved NaOH = 0.08 g / 40 g/mol = 0.002 mol.
• C₂ = [OH⁻] = 0.002 mol / 0.1 L = 0.02 mol/L.
• [H₃O⁺] = K_w / [OH⁻] = 10⁻¹⁴ / 0.02 = 5.0 × 10⁻¹³ mol/L.
• pH = -log(5.0 × 10⁻¹³) = 12.3 [4 points]
3. Neutralization Mixture Analysis:
• Net Ionic Equation: H₃O⁺(aq) + OH⁻(aq) → 2H₂O(l) [1 point]
• Initial Moles in Mixture:
- n(H₃O⁺) = C₁ × V₁ = 0.01 mol/L × 0.200 L = 0.002 mol.
- n(OH⁻) = C₂ × V₂ = 0.02 mol/L × 0.100 L = 0.002 mol. [1 point]
• Conclusion: Since n(H₃O⁺) = n(OH⁻) = 0.002 mol, the acid and base completely and exactly neutralize each other without any excess strong species. The resulting solution is perfectly Neutral (pH = 7.0 at 25°C). [2 points]
