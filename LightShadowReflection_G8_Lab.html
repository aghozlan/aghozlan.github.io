Exercise 1 — Light Sources

Identify luminous objects and properties of light. (2 pts)

Q1 · Which object is a luminous source of light?
✅ Correct! The Sun produces its own light — it is luminous.
❌ The Moon and mirror only reflect light — they are non-luminous.
Q2 · Light travels in…
✅ Correct! Light travels in straight lines — this is called rectilinear propagation.
❌ Light always travels in straight lines (rectilinear propagation) in a uniform medium.

Exercise 2 — Shadow Formation

Study the diagram below and answer the questions. (3 pts + journal)

🖼 Shadow Scene — Study this diagram
Q1 · Where does the shadow form?
✅ Correct! The shadow is a dark region on the screen where light is blocked by the opaque object.
❌ The shadow always forms on the opposite side of the light source, behind the opaque object.
Q2 · Which statement correctly describes the shadow diagram?
✅ Correct! The umbra is the region of total darkness in a shadow.
❌ The umbra is the fully dark region of a shadow where no light reaches at all.
📓 Lab Journal — Observation (teacher-read)

Describe in 2–3 sentences: what happens to the shadow's size when you move the light source closer to the object?

Q3 · A small point-source lamp creates a shadow. The shadow edge is…
✅ Correct! A point source creates only a pure umbra — no partial shadow (penumbra).
❌ A point source has no width, so it produces only a sharp-edged umbra, no penumbra.

Q1 · What does an arrow on a light ray represent?
✅ Correct! Arrows on rays show the direction of light travel.
❌ In ray diagrams, arrows always show the direction in which the light is travelling.
Q2 · Why do we draw light as straight lines with arrows?
✅ Correct! Straight lines reflect rectilinear propagation of light.
❌ We draw straight lines because light propagates in straight lines through uniform media.
Q3 · When a ray of light hits an opaque object, it…
✅ Correct! Opaque objects block light, forming a shadow.
❌ Opaque objects absorb or reflect light — rays cannot pass through, so a shadow forms behind.

Exercise 4 — Reflection & the Protractor

Measure the angle of reflection, then answer questions. (4 pts)

📐 Protractor Task — Click on the canvas to draw the reflected ray
The mirror is the horizontal line. The normal is shown dashed vertically. The incident ray arrives from the upper-left. Click anywhere above the mirror on the right side to set your reflected ray, then click Check Angle.
💡 Hint: The law of reflection says angle of incidence = angle of reflection. Both angles are measured from the normal (the dashed vertical line), not from the mirror surface.
Q1 · The law of reflection states that the angle of incidence…
✅ Correct! Angle of incidence = angle of reflection — this is the law of reflection.
❌ The law of reflection: angle of incidence = angle of reflection (both measured from the normal).
Q2 · Angles of incidence and reflection are measured from…
✅ Correct! The normal is the reference line — always perpendicular to the mirror surface.
❌ Both angles are measured from the normal — the imaginary line perpendicular to the mirror.
Q3 · A ray hits a flat mirror at 30° to the normal. The reflected ray makes an angle of ___ with the normal.
✅ Correct! 30° in = 30° out, by the law of reflection.
❌ The angle of reflection equals the angle of incidence, so 30° → 30°.

Exercise 5 — MCQ & Reflection Labelling

Three multiple-choice questions plus a diagram labelling task. (4 pts)

MCQ 1 · Which surface reflects light most effectively?
✅ Correct! Smooth, polished surfaces produce specular (regular) reflection.
❌ Polished/smooth surfaces give specular reflection — the most effective type of reflection.
MCQ 2 · Diffuse (scattered) reflection occurs on…
✅ Correct! Rough surfaces scatter light in many directions — diffuse reflection.
❌ Rough/matte surfaces scatter light in random directions — this is diffuse reflection.
MCQ 3 · The image formed by a plane (flat) mirror is…
✅ Correct! A plane mirror produces an upright, virtual, laterally-inverted image the same size as the object.
❌ A plane mirror gives an upright, virtual image — you cannot project it on a screen.
