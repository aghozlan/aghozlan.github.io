Exercise 1: Direct Current & Electric Power(5 points)
A lamp \((L)\) carries the inscriptions: \((12 \text{ V} ; 6 \text{ W})\). It is connected in series with an ohmic resistor \((R)\) of resistance \(R = 10 \ \Omega\), a closed switch \((K)\), and a DC generator \((G)\) maintaining a constant voltage \(U_G = 17 \text{ V}\).
  1. Give the physical significance of the inscriptions \((12 \text{ V} ; 6 \text{ W})\) on the lamp.
  2. Calculate the rated current \(I_R\) of the lamp.
  3. The current flowing in the circuit is \(I = 0.5 \text{ A}\).
    1. Calculate the voltage \(U_R\) across the resistor \((R)\).
    2. Deduce the voltage \(U_L\) across the lamp.
    3. Does the lamp function normally? Justify your answer.
  4. Calculate the electric energy consumed by the lamp in \(30\) minutes. Express the result in Joules.

Exercise 2: Alternating Voltage — Lebanese Mains Supply(5 points)
An electrician connects an oscilloscope to the live (phase) and neutral terminals of a Lebanese household electrical outlet. The vertical sensitivity is set to \(S_v = 100 \text{V/div}\) and the time base is set to \(S_h = 5 \text{ms/div}\). The oscillogram displayed on the screen is shown in Figure 2.
  1. Name the type of voltage displayed on the oscilloscope.
  2. Using Figure 2, determine the maximum voltage \(U_{max}\) of this supply.
    1. Calculate the effective (RMS) voltage \(U_{eff}\).  (Take \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41\))
    2. A voltmeter is connected to the same live–neutral terminals. What value does it display? Justify.
  4. Determine the period \(T\) of this voltage in milliseconds, then convert it to seconds.
  5. Deduce the frequency \(f\). What is the standard frequency of the Lebanese electrical network? Is your result consistent?

Exercise 3: Spring–Mass System — Equilibrium and Hooke’s Law(5 points)
A solid \((S)\) of mass \(m = 200 \text{g}\) is suspended from the free end of a vertical spring whose natural (unstretched) length is \(L_0 = 15 \text{cm}\). When the solid is in equilibrium, the total length of the spring becomes \(L = 20 \text{cm}\). (Take \(g = 10 \text{N/kg}\))
  1. Name the two forces acting on \((S)\) and give, for each, its line of action and direction.
  2. Give the condition of equilibrium of a solid subjected to two forces.
  3. Calculate the weight \(W\) of the solid \((S)\). Deduce the magnitude of the tension \(T\) exerted by the spring on \((S)\).
    1. Determine the elongation \(\Delta L\) of the spring in this equilibrium position.
    2. Using Hooke’s Law, deduce the stiffness constant \(k\) of the spring. Show your working.
  5. A new object \((S')\) is attached to the same spring. The spring stretches by \(10\text{cm}\) from its natural length. Determine the mass \(m'\) of \((S')\).

Exercise 4: Principle of Interaction(5 points)
A magnet \((A)\) and an iron ball \((B)\) are placed near each other on a smooth horizontal table. They attract each other by a force of magnitude \(5 \text{ N}\).
  1. Give the principle of interaction.
  2. Let \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) be the force exerted by \((A)\) on \((B)\), and \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) be the force exerted by \((B)\) on \((A)\). Write the vector relation between these two forces.
  3. Deduce the magnitude of both forces.
  4. Redraw the Figure, and then represent each of the forces \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) and \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) to a scale of 1cm corresponds to 2 N.

Exercise 2: Alternating Voltage — Lebanese Mains Supply (5 points)
1. Type of voltage Alternating sinusoidal voltage. 0.5 pt
2. Maximum voltage \(U_{max}\) Amplitude (peak to centre axis) = 3.1 div (as labelled on Figure 2).
\(U_{max} = 3.1 \times S_v = 3.1 \times 100 = 310 \approx 311 \text{ V}\).		 1.25 pt
(0.5 reading + 0.75 formula)
3.a. Effective voltage \(U_{eff}\) \(U_{eff} = \dfrac{U_{max}}{\sqrt{2}} = \dfrac{311}{1.41} \approx 220 \text{ V}\). 0.75 pt
3.b. Voltmeter reading A voltmeter always measures the effective (RMS) value of the voltage.
\(\Rightarrow\) It displays \(U_{eff} \approx \mathbf{220 \text{ V}}\).		 0.25 pt
4. Period \(T\) One complete cycle spans \(x = 4\) div on the time axis.
\(T = x \times S_h = 4 \times 5 = 20 \text{ ms} = 0.020 \text{ s}\).		 1.25 pt
(0.75 reading + 0.5 conversion)
5. Frequency \(f\) \(f = \dfrac{1}{T} = \dfrac{1}{0.020} = 50 \text{ Hz}\).
Lebanese standard = 50 Hz. Result is consistent. ✓		 1.0 pt
Exercise 3: Spring–Mass System — Equilibrium and Hooke’s Law (5 points)
1. Condition of equilibrium The two forces must have the same line of action, opposite directions, and equal magnitudes. 1.0 pt
2. Forces on \((S)\) • Weight \(\vec{W}\): vertical line of action, directed downward.
• Spring tension \(\vec{T}\): vertical line of action, directed upward.		 1.0 pt
3. Weight and Tension \(m = 200 \text{ g} = 0.200 \text{ kg}\).
\(W = m \times g = 0.200 \times 10 = 2 \text{ N}\).
At equilibrium: \(\vec{W} + \vec{T} = \vec{0} \Rightarrow T = W = 2 \text{ N}\).		 1.0 pt
4.a. Elongation \(\Delta L\) \(\Delta L = L - L_0 = 20 - 15 = 5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}\). 0.75 pt
4.b. Stiffness \(k\) Hooke’s Law: \(T = k \times \Delta L \Rightarrow k = \dfrac{T}{\Delta L} = \dfrac{2}{0.05} = 40 \text{ N/m}\). 0.75 pt
5. New mass \(m'\) \(\Delta L' = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.10 \text{ m}\).
\(T' = k \times \Delta L' = 40 \times 0.10 = 4 \text{ N}\).
\(m' = T' / g = 4 / 10 = 0.4 \text{ kg} = \mathbf{400 \text{ g}}\).		 0.5 pt
Exercise 4: Principle of Interaction (5 points)
1. Principle When body A exerts a force on body B, body B simultaneously exerts a force on body A that is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. 1.0 pt
2. Vector Relation \(\vec{F}_{A/B} = -\vec{F}_{B/A}\) 1.0 pt
3. Magnitude \(F_{A/B} = F_{B/A} = 5 \text{ N}\) 1.0 pt
4. Drawing - \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) drawn starting from B directed towards A.
- \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) drawn starting from A directed towards B with each arrow's length is 2.5 cm.
