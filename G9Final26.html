Exercise 1: Direct Current & Electric Power(5 points)
A lamp \((L)\) carries the inscriptions: \((12 \text{ V} ; 6 \text{ W})\). It is connected in series with an ohmic resistor \((R)\) of resistance \(R = 10 \ \Omega\), a closed switch \((K)\), and a DC generator \((G)\) maintaining a constant voltage \(U_G = 17 \text{ V}\).
  1. Give the physical significance of the inscriptions \((12 \text{ V} ; 6 \text{ W})\) on the lamp.
  2. Calculate the rated current \(I_R\) of the lamp.
  3. The current flowing in the circuit is \(I = 0.5 \text{ A}\).
    1. Calculate the voltage \(U_R\) across the resistor \((R)\).
    2. Deduce the voltage \(U_L\) across the lamp.
    3. Does the lamp function normally? Justify your answer.
  4. Calculate the electric energy consumed by the lamp in \(30\) minutes. Express the result in Joules.

Exercise 2: Alternating Voltage(5 points)
An oscilloscope is used to visualize an alternating sinusoidal voltage. The vertical sensitivity is set to \(S_v = 5 \text{ V/div}\) and the horizontal sensitivity (time base) is set to \(S_h = 10 \text{ ms/div}\). The visualized waveform is shown in Figure 2.
  1. Name the type of voltage measured.
  2. Determine the maximum voltage \(U_m\) of this source by reading the oscillogram.
  3. Calculate the effective voltage \(U_{eff}\). (Take \(\sqrt{2} \approx 1.4\))
  4. Determine the period \(T\) of this voltage in milliseconds, then convert it to seconds.
  5. Deduce the frequency \(f\) of this voltage.
Figure 2: Oscilloscope Screen
Exercise 3: Hooke's Law & Equilibrium(5 points)
A solid \((S)\) of mass \(m = 200 \text{ g}\) is suspended from the free end of a vertical spring of un-stretched length \(L_0 = 15 \text{ cm}\) and stiffness \(k = 40 \text{ N/m}\). The solid is in equilibrium. (Take \(g = 10 \text{ N/kg}\)).
  1. Give the condition of equilibrium of a solid subjected to two forces.
  2. Name the two forces acting on \((S)\) and give their lines of action and directions.
  3. Determine the weight \(W\) of the object and deduce the magnitude of the tension \(T\) .
  4. Determine the elongation \(\Delta L\) of the spring in this case.
  5. A new mass \(m_2\) is suspended to the free end of the spring, the spring elongates by \(10 \text{ cm}\). Determine the new mass that was held by the spring.
Figure 3: Solid on Spring
Exercise 4: Principle of Interaction(5 points)
A magnet \((A)\) and an iron ball \((B)\) are placed near each other on a smooth horizontal table. They attract each other by a force of magnitude \(5 \text{ N}\).
  1. Give the principle of interaction.
  2. Let \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) be the force exerted by \((A)\) on \((B)\), and \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) be the force exerted by \((B)\) on \((A)\). Write the vector relation between these two forces.
  3. Deduce the magnitude of both forces.
  4. Redraw the Figure, and then represent each of the forces \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) and \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) to a scale of 1cm corresponds to 2 N.

Exercise 1: Direct Current & Electric Power (5 points)

(0.5 ea)
2. Nominal Current Formula: \(P = U \times I\) \(\Rightarrow\) \(I_n = P / U = 6 / 12 = 0.5 \text{ A}\). 1.0 pt
3.a. Voltage \(U_R\) Applying Ohm's law: \(U_R = R \times I = 10 \times 0.5 = 5 \text{ V}\). 0.75 pt
3.b. Voltage \(U_L\) Law of addition of voltages: \(U_G = U_R + U_L\) \(\Rightarrow\) \(U_L = 17 - 5 = 12 \text{ V}\). 0.75 pt
3.c. Lamp Function Yes, the lamp functions normally because the applied voltage equals its nominal voltage (\(12 \text{ V} = 12 \text{ V}\)), or because \(I = I_n\). 0.5 pt
4. Electric Energy \(t = 30 \times 60 = 1800 \text{ s}\). \(E = P \times t = 6 \times 1800 = 10,800 \text{ Joules}\). 1.0 pt
Exercise 2: Alternating Voltage (5 points)
1. Type of voltage Alternating Sinusoidal Voltage. 0.5 pt
2. Maximum \(U_m\) Amplitude \(y = 3 \text{ div}\) (half of peak-to-peak). \(U_m = y \times S_v = 3 \times 5 = 15 \text{ V}\). 1.25 pt
3. Effective \(U_{eff}\) \(U_{eff} = U_m / \sqrt{2} = 15 / 1.4 \approx 10.71 \text{ V}\). 1.0 pt
4. Period \(T\) \(x = 4 \text{ div}\). \(T = x \times S_h = 4 \times 10 = 40 \text{ ms}\).
Conversion: \(40 \text{ ms} = 0.04 \text{ s}\).		 1.25 pt
5. Frequency \(f\) \(f = 1 / T = 1 / 0.04 = 25 \text{ Hz}\). 1.0 pt
Exercise 3: Hooke's Law & Equilibrium (5 points)
1. Condition The two forces must have the same line of action, opposite directions, and equal magnitudes. 1.0 pt
2. Forces & details - Weight \(\vec{W}\) (vertical, downward).
- Tension of the spring \(\vec{T}\) (vertical, upward).		 1.0 pt
3. Weight & Tension \(W = m \times g = 0.2 \text{ kg} \times 10 = 2 \text{ N}\).
At equilibrium, \(\vec{W} + \vec{T} = \vec{0}\). Thus, \(T = W = 2 \text{ N}\).		 1.5 pt
4. Elongation Hooke's Law: \(\Delta L = T / k = 2 / 40 = 0.05 \text{ m}\) (or \(5 \text{ cm}\)). 0.5 pt
5. New mass \(m_2\) New tension \(T' = k \times \Delta L' = 40 \times 0.1 \text{ m} = 4 \text{ N}\).
At equilibrium \(W' = T' = 4 \text{ N}\) \(\Rightarrow m_2 = 4 / 10 = 0.4 \text{ kg} = 400 \text{ g}\).		 1.0 pt
Exercise 4: Principle of Interaction (5 points)
1. Principle When body A exerts a force on body B, body B simultaneously exerts a force on body A that is equal in magnitude and opposite in direction. 1.0 pt
2. Vector Relation \(\vec{F}_{A/B} = -\vec{F}_{B/A}\) 1.0 pt
3. Magnitude \(F_{A/B} = F_{B/A} = 5 \text{ N}\) 1.0 pt
4. Drawing - \(\vec{F}_{A/B}\) drawn starting from B directed towards A.
- \(\vec{F}_{B/A}\) drawn starting from A directed towards B with each arrow's length is 2.5 cm.
- Both vectors must have the same length.		 2.0 pt
